Design a split shaft-collar clamp bracket that supports a rotating auger tube
on a flat plate. All units millimetres. The auger tube axis is horizontal
along Y; the bracket stands up from a plate in the XY plane.

Flange base: a rectangular block 60.0 wide (X) by 12.0 deep (Y) by 14.0 tall
(Z), with two M3 clearance holes (diameter 3.4) drilled vertically through it
at X = -24 and X = +24 for bolting down to the plate.

Collar ring: a cylindrical ring rising on top of the flange, centred so its
bore axis is 29.25 mm above the bottom of the flange, ring outer diameter
33.5, bore diameter 25.5 (a 25.0 mm auger outer diameter plus 0.5 mm diametral
running clearance, so the auger spins freely). The collar axis runs along Y
through the ring.

Clamp slot: a thin 2.0 mm wide vertical slit cut through one side of the
collar ring (a split-collar gap) so a clamp screw can lightly set the running
fit.

Why it matters: the bracket holds the spinning auger concentric and sets its
height (axis at Z = 29.25 above the plate) while letting it rotate freely.
