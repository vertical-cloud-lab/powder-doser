Design a split collar that carries a vibration motor and a tap solenoid around
a powder auger tube. All units millimetres. The auger tube axis runs along Y
(through the collar bore); Z is up, X is sideways.

Collar body: a cylindrical ring, outer diameter 33.5, bore diameter 25.5 (a
25.0 mm auger outer diameter plus 0.5 mm running clearance so the collar spins
freely on the tube - it must NOT clamp tight), collar depth 17.0 along the Y
bore axis. A 2.0 mm wide clamp slot is cut through one side of the ring (only
to set a light running fit, never to grip).

Coin vibration motor pad: a shallow flat circular recess, diameter 10.0 and
1.0 mm deep, on the -X face of the collar, to bond a 10 mm coin vibration
motor.

Solenoid boss: on the +Z (top) face, a mounting boss for a push/pull solenoid
whose plunger points down along X toward the auger. A through bore of diameter
7.5 lets the plunger pass; two M3 clearance holes (diameter 3.4) on a
diagonal pattern 18.2 mm apart across the body and 16.0 mm along it mount the
solenoid flange.

Hardstop ear: a small tab sticking out radially on the +X side of the collar
that catches a fixed stop so the collar cannot rotate with the spinning auger.

Why it matters: the collar both vibrates and hammer-taps the tube wall to stop
powder bridging, while the hardstop ear keeps it from spinning with the auger
and winding up its own wires - so the bore is a free-spinning fit, never a clamp.
