Design the fixed baseplate of a powder doser that two servos sit on and the
tilting table hinges from. All units millimetres. Flat plate in the XY plane,
symmetric about X = 0; Z is up, plate bottom at Z = -14.

Base slab: thickness 6.0, a rectangular tab spanning X from -100 to +100
(200 wide) and Y from +55 to +115 (60 deep). The two rear corners (the +Y
corners) are each chamfered 25.0 by 45 degrees.

Mounting holes: four M5 clearance holes (diameter 5.4) near the four corners
at X = -80 and +80, Y = +68 and +105, clear of the chamfers, to bolt the
baseplate to an external frame.

Hinge arms: two arms, one each side, rising at the -Y front edge to carry the
tilt-axis M5 pins. The hinge axis is 10 mm forward of the baseplate front edge
at Z = 29.25; each hinge eye is outer diameter 18.0 with an M5 bore (diameter
5.4). Each arm is about 35 mm deep, and its -Y (motor-side) face is sloped at
about 58 degrees so the tilting plate can swing through 45 degrees and beyond
without hitting the arm.

Servo posts: two pairs of square posts, one pair each side, that the MG996R
servo flanges bolt to, with the servo body protruding through a 40 mm gap
between the posts; the servo spline axis sits 10.0 mm above the baseplate top.
Add underside flange ribs (one per servo) 6 mm thick at X = -79 and +79
dropping 40 mm below the baseplate bottom, each with a triangular gusset and a
horizontal M5 hole (diameter 5.4) 15 mm below the baseplate bottom for
side-bolting.

Why it matters: this is the fixed ground reference that bolts to the frame,
carries the two tilt servos, and reacts the tilt load through the hinge arms.
