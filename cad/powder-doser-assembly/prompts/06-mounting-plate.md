Design the tilting mounting plate ("the table") of a powder doser. All units
millimetres. It is a flat plate in the XY plane, symmetric about X = 0; Z is up.

Plate slab: thickness 6.0, spanning X from -54.1 to +54.1 (width about 108.2)
and Y from -15 to +115 (about 130 long).

Front U-notch: an open rectangular notch cut into the +Y front edge, 32.0 wide
in X (centred on X = 0), cut 35 mm back into the plate, so the auger can
overhang in mid-air. This notch also splits the two front ramps and the two
side hinges.

On the TOP surface only (nothing hangs below the plate), add mounting
features:
- Two front triangular ramp gussets, one each side of the U-notch.
- A stepper motor block: a boss at X = +32, about 36 by 36 footprint, 6 mm
  tall, whose +Y face carries the NEMA 11 motor bolt pattern.
- Two side hinge lobe stacks, one each side at the +Y end, each carrying a
  40-tooth gear band (module 0.9083, 20 degree pressure angle, pitch diameter
  about 36.33, tip diameter about 38.15) so two servo gears can lift the plate.
  The hinge bore is for an M5 pin (diameter 5.4), axis 29.25 mm above the plate
  top.

Why it matters: this is the moving table that aims the whole dispenser; it
tilts about the front hinge and keeps the auger, brackets, stepper and gears
all on one datum (auger axis 29.25 above the plate top).
