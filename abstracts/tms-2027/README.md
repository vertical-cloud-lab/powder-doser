# TMS 2027 — abstract

Submission to the **TMS 2027 Annual Meeting & Exhibition** (The Minerals,
Metals & Materials Society), March 14–18, 2027, Orlando World Center Marriott,
Orlando, FL. Abstracts are submitted through ProgramMaster
(<https://www.programmaster.org/TMS2027>) with a **150-word** body limit; the
call-for-abstracts deadline is **July 1, 2026**.

[`abstract.md`](abstract.md) contains the abstract (150 words). It shares the
same scope as the companion MRS Fall 2026 and Utah AI Convergence 2026
submissions ([`../mrs-fall-2026/abstract.md`](../mrs-fall-2026/abstract.md),
[`../utah-ai-convergence-2026/abstract.md`](../utah-ai-convergence-2026/abstract.md)):
the open-source, low-cost powder doser; proposal-anchored hardware targets
(PR #27); progression from non-hazardous powders to L-PBF feedstocks; and the
engineer-led, AI-modeled CAD workflow. Framing is tuned to the TMS
metallurgy/materials audience by foregrounding the doser hardware and its role
in an autonomous alloy-discovery pipeline, with the agentic-AI / generative-CAD
assessment as a secondary thread.

## Target symposium

TMS 2027 organizes its program into 11 technical tracks and 100+ symposia. The
best-aligned symposia for this work are, in order:

1. **AI-Enabled Materials Processing: Integrating Accelerated Experimental
   Workflows and Processing-Aware Machine Learning** (Data-Driven and
   Computational Materials Design track) — **primary.** This symposium
   explicitly welcomes autonomous and accelerated experimental workflows,
   closed-loop / Bayesian-optimization experimentation, and the instrumentation
   that enables them. The doser is exactly such an enabling instrument: it
   meters and blends feedstock for a self-driving, Bayesian-optimization
   alloy-discovery loop, with an ML calibration algorithm mapping actuator
   settings to dispensed mass.
2. **Additive Manufacturing and Innovative Feedstock Processing for
   Multifunctional Materials** (Additive Manufacturing track) — *alternate.*
   Directly on-topic for the powder/feedstock-handling contribution and its link
   to L-PBF feedstock quality and reproducibility.
3. **AI/ML/Data Informatics for Materials Discovery: Bridging Experiment,
   Theory, and Modeling** (Data-Driven and Computational Materials Design
   track) — *alternate.* Fits the data-driven, accelerated-discovery framing and
   the AI-assisted design study.

Submit the abstract to only one symposium. No content was added that is not
already grounded in this repository's issues, PRs, or proposal (PR #27).
