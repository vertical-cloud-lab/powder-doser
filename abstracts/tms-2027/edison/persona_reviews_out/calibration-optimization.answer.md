=== ABSTRACT UNDER REVIEW (150-word body) ===
Title: Calibration Optimization of an Auger-Based Powder Doser as a Probe of Powder Flow Behavior, with Physics-Based Simulation to Infer Physical Properties

Dispensed mass from an auger-based powder doser depends on actuator settings and powder properties, so every calibration curve doubles as an indirect probe of powder flow behavior. We frame calibration of an open-source doser—combining stepper-driven auger rotation, solenoid tapping, vibration-motor agitation, and servo-controlled tilt—as an AI-driven, multi-objective, multi-task optimization problem suited to multi-task Bayesian optimization. The competing objectives are dose accuracy, repeatability, dispensing time, and minimum and maximum deliverable dose. Each powder—progressing from non-hazardous surrogates to laser powder bed fusion feedstocks such as AlSi10Mg, silicon, and stainless steel—is a related task, and multi-task models share information across powders to cut per-powder calibration effort. Alongside gravimetric parameter sweeps, we are exploring physics-based simulations of powder conveying and agitation (e.g., discrete element modeling) that connect dosing response to cohesion, friction, and particle size distribution, enabling inference of powder properties from calibration data and simulation-seeded calibration of new powders.

## 5. Consensus Summary

The panel's consensus is that the abstract describes genuinely interesting work that sits at the boundary of this symposium's scope. The core idea—using an instrumented doser as a compact experimental probe of powder flow mechanisms, with DEM to invert dosing data for physical properties and multi-task Bayesian optimization to share information across powders—aligns with the symposium's interests in advanced characterization, computational modeling, and data-driven discovery in powder systems. However, the current text reads more like a proposal for **equipment calibration with AI** than a contribution to **fundamental powder-processing science**.

The single most impactful revision is to **reframe the contribution**: the doser is a means, not the end; the end is mechanistic understanding of powder flow behavior and transferable physics-based models. The second most impactful revision is to **replace the hardware inventory** with scientific content—specifically, what properties are being inferred, how they will be validated, and what they imply for downstream powder processing. Together, these two changes would shift the abstract from borderline to solid scope fit without changing the underlying research.

The claims about DEM-based property inference, multi-task transfer across powders, and simulation-seeded calibration of new powders are scientifically plausible and grounded in existing literature on transfer-learning Bayesian optimization (wagner2025sampleefficientbayesiantransfer pages 5-6, bai2023transferlearningfor pages 22-24) and powder flow characterization (manokaran2024effectofmicroscopic pages 9-11, manokaran2024effectofmicroscopic pages 6-9), but each currently lacks stated evidence or a validation pathway in the abstract. The presenter should be prepared to address these gaps during the Q&A at minimum, or ideally resolve them in the revised abstract text.
