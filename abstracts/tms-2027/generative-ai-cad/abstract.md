Title: Agentic AI and Generative CAD for Laboratory Hardware: Lessons from Developing an Open-Source Powder Doser

Agentic AI coding tools and generative "text-to-CAD" services promise to accelerate laboratory hardware development, yet their practical capabilities remain poorly characterized. We report a practitioner-level assessment from developing an open-source, low-cost powder doser for laser powder bed fusion (L-PBF) feedstock research. We evaluated coding agents and hosted generative-CAD services—CADSmith, zoo.dev, and GitHub Copilot Cloud Agents with Claude Opus 4.7—to generate and revise parametric CAD in CadQuery and OpenSCAD from engineering requirements. Fully AI-generated designs were not deployment-ready, so we adopted an engineer-led workflow: engineers defined part geometry, dimensions, hand sketches, and manufacturing constraints; AI modeled the parts; engineers reviewed, printed, and iterated. Specificity—explicit constraints and drawings of exactly what was wanted—substantially improved results, yet usable geometry still required many correction cycles, with weak spatial reasoning a recurring limitation. We present AI-modeled prototypes, a comparison of attempted workflows, and practical guidance for AI-assisted instrument design.
