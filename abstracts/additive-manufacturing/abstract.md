Title: An Open-Source Autonomous Multi-Powder Doser for High-Throughput AM Alloy Discovery

Autonomous discovery of new multi-component alloys for additive manufacturing (AM) demands precise, repeatable dispensing of elemental and pre-alloyed powders across a wide composition space — a capability that has remained a manual bottleneck in high-throughput AM workflows. Here we present an open-source, modular multi-powder doser designed for self-driving-laboratory (SDL) integration, capable of autonomously dispensing up to 50 unique powder feedstocks via interchangeable auger mechanisms with closed-loop gravimetric feedback. We report calibration results across a diverse set of AM alloy-relevant powder classes, including titanium- and aluminum-based systems, nickel superalloy precursors, refractory high-entropy alloy blends, and stainless steel compositions, spanning a range of particle morphologies, size distributions, and flowability regimes. Calibration accuracy and repeatability are characterized as a function of target mass, auger geometry, and powder properties, yielding a predictive model that generalizes across feedstock classes. The instrument hardware is generated through an LLM-assisted parametric CAD pipeline, enabling rapid adaptation of auger and hopper geometry to new powder characteristics. Integrated into an SDL closed-loop optimization loop, the doser enables automated traversal of multi-dimensional alloy composition spaces with print-ready powder blends delivered on demand. We demonstrate its utility in an autonomous AM alloy discovery campaign and discuss design guidelines for extending the platform to additional feedstock chemistries. All hardware designs, firmware, calibration software, and datasets are released as open-source resources to lower the barrier to entry for autonomous AM alloy research.
