## Narrative role of Fig. 5 (`fig5_future`) and recommendation

### What the figure is

Two side-by-side panels generated by `make_figures.py::fig5()`:

- **(a) "N-channel radial array (concept)"** — a matplotlib cartoon: 8 light-blue rectangles arranged around a beige circle labelled "shared cup". It is *not* a CAD render; the panel is built in code (`patches.Rectangle … patches.Circle … ax.text(0, 0, "shared\ncup", …)` in the `fig5` function of `make_figures.py`). No part of this geometry comes from the design repo.
- **(b) "Inward-tilting channels over shared cup (CAD)"** — a single static PNG (`assets/inward_collection_cup_iso.png`) imported via `show(ax, "inward_collection_cup_iso.png")`. The source CAD lives on one branch only (`origin/copilot/brainstorming-design-possibilities`, `design/cad/inward-collection-cup/{cad_model.py,inward_collection_cup.step,…}`). The figure shows the *cup geometry*, not a channel arrayed around it.

Caption (`caption_fig5_future.md`): "Future work: multi-powder doser. (a) N-channel radial array concept around a shared collection cup on a single balance. (b) CAD study of inward-tilting channels over the shared cup."

### Job the figure does in the paper's argument

The manuscript flags a multi-powder doser as the next stage in three places:

1. Intro/objectives, p. 2: "establish the modular foundation … for the multi-powder doser, automated calibration, and powder-property database that constitute the subsequent stages of this program."
2. §2.1 platform overview, p. 2: "each channel is an independent printable unit that will later be arrayed around a shared collection cup to form a multi-powder doser (Section 4)." — note that this forward-pointer is to the **Conclusions section**, not to Fig. 5.
3. §4 Conclusions, p. 7, ongoing-work list, item (1): "a multi-powder doser arraying N printed channels around a shared collection cup (Fig. 5), with a loaded subset of 8–12 powders and a path to a 50-powder pool via swappable sealed cartridges".

Fig. 5 is therefore meant to give the reader a one-glance picture of stage (1) of the five-stage roadmap. It is the only figure tied to §4 (Fig. 1–3 belong to §2.1–2.3, Fig. 4 to §2.4).

### Whether the role is coherent and non-redundant

**Coherence — weak.** The figure under-delivers on what the surrounding text promises.

- Panel (a) is a stylised cartoon (eight rectangles around a circle) that conveys exactly the same information as the sentence in §4 that cites it. The "N-channel radial array" is depicted with N hard-coded to 8 in code, while the text talks about 8–12 channels and a 50-powder pool via swappable cartridges. Cartridges, the actual novel mechanism mentioned in the same sentence, are not shown.
- Panel (b) is captioned "inward-tilting channels over the shared cup", but the render contains *no channels*: it is the collection cup alone. The depicted geometry doesn't match its label. A reader expecting to see how channels tilt inward toward a shared cup will only see the cup.
- The §2.1 forward-reference for the array concept goes to "Section 4", not to Fig. 5, so Fig. 5 is decoupled from the only sentence in Results that introduces the modular vision. Fig. 1c already establishes the "tilt about a fixed dispense point" idea that panel (b) is implicitly leaning on, so panel (b) reads as a redundant restatement of an idea Fig. 1c made better with the single channel.
- The PR review explicitly warns that this figure is premature. `pr97_comments.md`, thread @ `paper/main.tex` line 258 (Future work), @swcharles: "The future work has changed a bit, but progress here has not yet been reported on Github. This section and its figures will be changed as updated information is uploaded."

**Repository support — thin.** Across all 45 branches in `all_branches_file_tree.txt`:

- No CAD anywhere shows multiple channels around a shared cup. Searches for `radial`, `array`, `multi-channel`, `multi-powder`, `manifold`, `shared_cup` return zero hits in `design/cad/**`.
- The only multi-doser-named branch is `origin/copilot/zoo-design-studio-multi-doser`, and its only relevant artefacts are prose notes (`paper/background/15-zoo-design-studio-multi-doser.md`, `paper/background/23-zds-transcript-editing-session.md`) — no CAD parts.
- The inward cup is a one-off study on the brainstorming branch and has not been integrated with any channel module.
- This undercuts the manuscript's stronger claim in §4: "the multi-doser geometry and the cartridge-sealing concepts already have working CAD in the repository." A single `cartridge.stl` exists under `design/cad/single-channel-module/stl/`, and the inward cup exists in isolation, but a multi-doser *geometry* does not.

**Redundancy — partial.** Panel (a) restates one sentence of §4 with no added geometric or quantitative content. Panel (b) overlaps with Fig. 1c's tilt-axis story and with §3.4's coarse-then-trickle protocol diagram in spirit but adds no new mechanism.

### Recommendation — move to SI (with caveats), and re-scope before resubmission

Cutting it entirely is defensible given the @swcharles review note that "this section and its figures will be changed". Keeping it as a main-text figure is hard to justify: it is the only main figure that is (i) part cartoon and part watermark-free CAD-of-the-wrong-thing, (ii) not anchored in Results, and (iii) not backed by repository CAD for its central claim (radial array). Merging into Fig. 1 would conflate the demonstrated single-channel module with speculative future geometry, which would muddy the paper's actual contribution.

Moving Fig. 5 to the SI preserves the roadmap framing in §4 without overcommitting on geometry that does not yet exist in the repo, and matches how the paper already handles other speculative or in-progress material (placeholder data in Fig. 4 is watermarked SYNTHETIC; nozzle variants are in SI Fig. S1).

1. **Move Fig. 5 to the SI** (rename `figS2_future_multi_doser`), and replace the in-text Fig. 5 citation in §4 with a sentence pointer ("see SI Fig. S2"). Rationale: PR thread on line 258 says the future-work section and its figures will change; the figure is not anchored in Results; repo CAD does not yet support the N-channel claim.
2. **Fix the panel (b) caption/label mismatch.** The asset shows the cup, not "inward-tilting channels over [the] cup". Either re-render with a representative channel tilted over the cup (the inward-cup `cad_model.py` on `origin/copilot/brainstorming-design-possibilities` plus the single-channel module on `origin/copilot/draft-base-manuscript` can be composed), or relabel the panel honestly as "shared collection cup (CAD study)".

Rewrite as "early CAD studies of the shared cup and a sealed cartridge exist in the repository (`design/cad/inward-collection-cup/`, `design/cad/single-channel-module/stl/cartridge.stl`)".

5. **Add a cross-reference from §2.1** ("…will later be arrayed around a shared collection cup to form a multi-powder doser") to the figure (currently it points only to "Section 4"), if the figure is retained. If demoted to SI, leave the §2.1 pointer at "Section 4" and let the conclusions section carry the SI reference.

### Discretionary decisions

- Recommended SI placement rather than outright deletion, on the grounds that the five-stage roadmap is part of the paper's stated contribution (objective 3, p. 2) and removing the figure would leave §4's stage-(1) sentence un-illustrated. A reviewer who values keeping speculative work out of an open-hardware Full Paper could equivalently justify a hard cut.
- Treated panel (a) as a cartoon rather than a CAD render based on the `make_figures.py` source (matplotlib `patches.Rectangle` and `Circle`), not on visual inspection alone.
- Interpreted §4's "(Fig. 5)" as the only Fig. 5 callout because the §2.1 forward-pointer is to "Section 4" rather than to the figure number; a stricter reading that counts the §2.1 sentence as an indirect citation would slightly strengthen the case for keeping the figure in main text.
- Used the 45-branch file tree, not a live `git` query, to assess whether multi-doser CAD exists; a branch added after the snapshot would not appear here.
