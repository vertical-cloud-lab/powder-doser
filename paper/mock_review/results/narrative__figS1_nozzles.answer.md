## Figure S1 (figS1_nozzles) — narrative role review

### What the figure is

A 1×4 strip of CAD cross-sections showing four printed exit-nozzle variants of the test auger (`nozzle_type{1..4}_cross_section.png`, generated by `make_figures.py:figs1()`; source SCAD/STL in `cad/auger-geared/archimedes-auger-test-nozzle{1..4}.{scad,stl}` per the branch tree). The caption (`caption_figS1_nozzles.md`) states only that the variants differ in "exit constriction and chamfer geometry." Visually, Types 1 and 2 are nearly identical blunt cylindrical exits; Types 3 and 4 are conical/chamfered tips of differing taper. No dimensions, parameter values, taper angles, exit-bore diameters, or per-type design rationale are given on the figure or in the caption.

### The job the figure is meant to do, and where it slots into the argument

The manuscript invokes the nozzles twice, both as forward-looking pointers rather than as evidence:

1. **Main text §2.3 (page 4):** "Exit-nozzle geometry strongly affects flow initiation and dribble; four printed nozzle variants are compared in SI Fig. S1." This sentence sits inside the "Design specifics" Fig. 2 discussion, not inside dispensing results.
2. **SI §S3 (page 2):** "Four printed exit-nozzle geometries were produced for dispense testing (Fig. S1); the dispensing campaign will report flow-initiation and dribble behaviour per nozzle and per powder."

So the figure's stated job is to *document the hardware variants that a future characterization campaign will compare*. The campaign itself does not exist yet — main-paper Fig. 4 is explicitly a watermarked synthetic placeholder ("SYNTHETIC placeholder data, to be replaced by bench measurements"), there are no per-nozzle data files anywhere in the branch tree (no `data/.../nozzle*.csv`), and SI §S3 uses future tense.

A third, implicit role is methodological: the main-text anecdote in §2.3.0.2 about "four miniature test nozzles with two of the four silently swapped relative to their documentation" almost certainly refers to these same four variants (file naming in `cad/auger-geared/archimedes-auger-test-nozzle{1..4}` matches; `edison_run_nozzles.py` and `edison_artifacts/nozzle-selection.*` indicate this was a discrete generative-AI sub-task). Fig. S1 could anchor that anecdote concretely — but the current figure and caption never make that link.

### Is the role coherent and non-redundant?

Partially. Concerns, ranked:

- **Coherence is weak.** A reader following the §2.3 pointer expects to learn what distinguishes the four geometries and why these four were chosen. The figure shows the four side by side, but the caption says only "differing in exit constriction and chamfer geometry" — no numbers, no labels of what's varied (bore Ø, chamfer angle, lip thickness, internal taper). Without that, Fig. S1 cannot answer the question the main-text sentence raises.
- **Non-redundancy with Fig. 2 is borderline.** Main-paper Fig. 2a is a cross-section of the printed auger tube (`auger_geared_cross_section.png`). The four Fig. S1 panels each show essentially the same full auger cross-section — most of the picture area in S1 is auger flighting that is identical between panels. Only the bottom ~20–25 % (the tip) actually differs (visible in the tip crop generated from the supplied assets). The figure repeats a lot of Fig. 2a real estate to show small distinguishing features.
- **It points to a campaign that hasn't run.** SI §S3 promises per-nozzle flow-initiation and dribble data; none of that exists in the manuscript or the branch tree. For RSC Digital Discovery, a Full Paper that ships hardware variants without the comparison they exist to support invites reviewer pushback that the figure is scope-creeping into future work — the same concern @swcharles flags for the future-work section in PR #97 ("This section and its figures will be changed as updated information is uploaded").
- **A genuinely useful narrative role is available but unused.** The "two of four silently swapped" anecdote in §2.3.0.2 is one of the paper's most concrete pieces of methodological evidence and currently has no figure. Fig. S1 is the natural place to ground it (annotate which two were swapped, in which iteration, and how the human review caught it). That would convert S1 from a parts catalogue into a worked example of the verification burden the paper argues is the dominant cost of the workflow.

### Recommendation: keep in SI, but rework

Do not promote to the main text (no data to show; would duplicate Fig. 2a's framing). Do not cut (the variants exist on disk, they're cited in the AI-workflow argument, and removing them would orphan the §2.3 sentence and weaken the silent-swap anecdote). Move-to-main would be premature.

**Keep in SI with three concrete fixes:**

1. **Crop and annotate.** Show only the tip region (last ~25 % of each cross-section) at larger scale, with arrows/labels for the varied parameters (exit bore, taper angle, chamfer, internal lip). The full-length flighting carries no information across panels and should be a single inset reference image, not repeated four times.
2. **Caption the design intent and the parameter values.** Replace "differing in exit constriction and chamfer geometry" with the actual parametric values used in each `.scad` file (these exist in `cad/auger-geared/archimedes-auger-test-nozzle{1..4}.scad`) and one sentence per variant on the hypothesised flow effect, cross-referencing the Edison `nozzle-selection.answer.md` artefact already on the branch.
3. **Tie to the §2.3 silent-swap anecdote.** Add a short SI §S3 paragraph (or a panel label) identifying which two of the four were the swapped pair and how the swap was caught. This is the strongest narrative justification the figure has in the current draft and costs almost nothing to add.

If the bench campaign won't land before submission, also add an explicit line in SI §S3 that S1 documents the variants printed to date and that per-nozzle performance will be reported in the multi-doser follow-up — matching the same framing used for the design-log quantitative analysis in §2.3.

### Prioritised action list

1. **Rewrite caption** to specify what varies between Types 1–4 with values from the SCAD sources (`cad/auger-geared/archimedes-auger-test-nozzle*.scad`). Highest impact, lowest cost.
2. **Crop panels to the tip region** and add geometric annotations; keep one small full-auger inset for orientation. Removes the Fig. 2a redundancy.
3. **Connect to §2.3.0.2 silent-swap anecdote** by naming the swapped pair in the SI text and/or panel labels.
4. **Add forward-pointer sentence in SI §S3** stating that the comparative dispensing data are deferred to the multi-doser follow-up, mirroring the deferral language already used for the design-log analysis.
5. **Leave figure in SI.** Do not merge, move to main, or cut.

### Discretionary decisions

- Treated Fig. S1's primary role as "document variants for future campaign" based on the SI §S3 future-tense wording; an equally defensible reading is "support the §2.3 sentence on exit-nozzle effects," and the recommendation would be similar under either reading.
- Judged the four panels as substantially redundant with Fig. 2a based on visual inspection of the supplied PNGs (≈75–80 % of pixel area is non-differentiating flighting). A reviewer with a stricter redundancy bar might recommend merging S1 into a single multi-tip composite panel; a more lenient bar might leave it alone.
- Assumed the §2.3.0.2 "four miniature test nozzles" anecdote refers to the same four variants based on file-naming (`archimedes-auger-test-nozzle{1..4}`) and the `edison_run_nozzles.py` script on the branch; this is not stated explicitly in the manuscript.
- Recommended "keep in SI with rework" rather than "cut pending data" because the variants are tied to a methodological anecdote, not only to the deferred campaign; a reviewer prioritising data-completeness over narrative might cut until campaign data exist.
- Did not recommend specific annotation styles (callout vs. dimension lines vs. tabular caption); any of these would address the missing-parameters concern.
