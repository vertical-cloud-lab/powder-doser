# State-of-the-Art Landscape of Generative CAD and Generative Design Tools and Research (2025)

## 1. Introduction

The landscape of generative CAD encompasses three increasingly convergent domains: (i) commercial topology-optimization (TO) and generative-design (GD) platforms, (ii) parametric/code-based CAD frameworks enabling scriptable and CI/CD-friendly workflows, and (iii) a rapidly growing body of AI/LLM-driven CAD generation research. This report surveys each domain, summarizes benchmark results, and identifies cross-cutting constraints around manufacturability, editability, parametric controllability, and scriptability.

---

## 2. Commercial Topology-Optimization and Generative-Design Products

Modern commercial GD tools share a common four-stage workflow: define parameters and objectives (typically via topology optimization), generate design alternatives, evaluate/iterate with simulation (FEA/CAE), and select for manufacturing (belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 11-14). Most tools implement density-based methods (SIMP with penalization ρᵖ) or Level Set approaches using Hamilton–Jacobi boundary evolution, producing organic, lightweight, performance-ranked geometries (belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 30-34). Key trends include the shift from finding a single optimal solution to generating multiple design alternatives, tighter integration with additive manufacturing (DfAM) constraints, and the emerging use of machine learning (GANs, reinforcement learning) to guide or filter outputs (souvanhnakhoomman2025acomprehensivereview pages 2-3, belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 34-37).

**Autodesk Fusion Generative Design** supports multi-outcome topology-optimization-driven exploration with integrated simulation validation. Case studies in the literature demonstrate mass reductions (e.g., 705 g to 586 g) and performance gains through concurrent generation and evaluation of many design iterations (souvanhnakhoomman2025acomprehensivereview pages 5-6). **Siemens NX Topology Optimizer**, integrated with Simcenter HEEDS and Simcenter CAE, enables rapid exploration of hundreds to thousands of optimized designs with end-to-end optimization and manufacturing validation (souvanhnakhoomman2025acomprehensivereview pages 5-6). **nTopology (nTop)** differentiates through implicit/field-driven modeling and lattice design, making it particularly strong for AM-oriented workflows. **PTC Creo Generative Design**, **ANSYS Discovery**, and **Altair Inspire** each embed TO within their respective simulation-first or parametric-CAD-first ecosystems. **Rhino + Grasshopper** provides unparalleled flexibility through its algorithmic-design plugin ecosystem (Galapagos, Wallacei, Karamba) but places more burden on users to enforce manufacturability and engineering governance (belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 11-14, belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 34-37).

**Common limitations across commercial GD/TO tools** include: (1) organic/asymmetric outputs that are difficult to manufacture conventionally; (2) sensitivity to design-space definition, which can yield sub-optimal solutions; (3) post-processing (smoothing, reconstruction) that may weaken embedded manufacturing constraints; (4) computational expense for large searches; and (5) the persistent need for human-in-the-loop review to select and refine usable designs from many alternatives (belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 30-34, kharche2025topologyoptimizationin pages 8-9).

## 3. Parametric / Code-Based CAD Frameworks

Code-based CAD frameworks are increasingly important for reproducible, version-controlled design pipelines and as targets for LLM-based CAD generation. Among these, **CadQuery** (Python, OpenCascade backend) stands out as offering "the best trade-off between usability and flexibility, making it well-suited for programmatic shape synthesis" with excellent Jupyter/scripting integration, no external software requirements, and straightforward CI/CD integration (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). CadQuery requires less code than OpenSCAD to define models and supports standard exports (STEP, STL) for manufacturing workflows (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 40-44). However, OpenCascade numerical robustness issues can surface with near-colinear arcs or very small-scale geometries (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 15-18).

**OpenSCAD** uses its own declarative DSL (e.g., `cube([10,10,10]);`) and is deterministic and parametric, but offers weaker ecosystem integration and less convenient automation than Python-native frameworks (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). **build123d** is a newer Python-first framework also built on OpenCascade, designed for fully scriptable workflows with strong typing and composability, inheriting CadQuery's CI/CD strengths while offering a more modern API. **Onshape FeatureScript** operates as a TypeScript-like language within the Onshape cloud platform, enabling custom parametric features with full regeneration logic and constraint enforcement, though it is tied to the vendor platform. **JSCAD** (JavaScript) is browser/npm-oriented and useful for web-based generation but offers weaker exact-CAD interoperability for professional MCAD/PLM workflows.

A critical finding is that LLM-generated code across these frameworks can differ between runs, which increases variance and can harm the reliability required for CI/CD pipelines (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 40-44). Effective use of LLM-generated parametric CAD scripts still requires technical skill for modification and troubleshooting.

## 4. AI/LLM-Driven CAD Generation Research

The AI-driven CAD generation field has expanded dramatically since 2021, evolving from autoregressive sequence models to LLM-based agents and multimodal diffusion systems.

### 4.1 Foundational Sequence Models

**DeepCAD** (Wu et al., 2021) models CAD creation as a sequence of operations and uses a Transformer-based architecture trained on over 170,000 CAD models with construction steps. It produces fully parametric models with clean geometric features (flat planar faces, cylindrical holes) that can be opened and edited in CAD software (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 21-25). On standard benchmarks, DeepCAD achieves CD = 4.25, HD = 39.25, and an Invalid Ratio (IR) of 7.14% (lin2025asurveyon pages 14-16). Limitations include inability to generate features requiring current model state references (e.g., fillets) and increasing topological invalidity with sequence complexity (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 25-29).

### 4.2 Text-Conditioned CAD Generation

**Text2CAD** (Khan et al., 2024, NeurIPS) trains a transformer to map text prompts to CAD construction sequences using LLM+VLM-annotated datasets. It achieves CD median = 0.370, IR = 3.5%, and Gemini visual-match accuracy of 58.8% (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 6-8). Limitations stem from dataset bias toward simple primitives and exclusion of certain CAD features (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 25-29).

**Text-to-CadQuery** (Xie & Ju, 2025) reframes text-to-CAD by generating executable CadQuery Python code directly, leveraging pretrained LLM capabilities. Fine-tuning six open-source LLMs (124M–7B parameters) on ~170K text–CadQuery pairs, the best model (Qwen2.5-3B) achieves a top-1 exact match of 69.3% (up from 58.8%) and a 48.6% reduction in Chamfer Distance compared to Text2CAD, with a Gemini visual-match score of 69.30%. Training completed in 58 minutes versus 2 days for Text2CAD, and larger pretrained models consistently yield better results (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 1-3, xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 6-8).

### 4.3 LLM-Based Approaches

**CAD-LLM** (Wu et al., 2023) fine-tunes pretrained language models (T5, 770M parameters) on the SketchGraphs dataset for sketch autocompletion, achieving Entity Accuracy = 0.689, Sketch Accuracy = 0.225, and CAD F1 = 0.440, outperforming both Vitruvion and fine-tuned ChatGPT baselines (wu2023cadllmlargelanguage pages 1-3).

**CAD-Llama** (Li et al., 2025, CVPR) introduces a hierarchical annotation pipeline and Structured Parametric CAD Code (SPCC) format, combined with adaptive pretraining and instruction tuning of LLMs. On the DeepCAD test set, it achieves ACCT = 84.72, MCD = 10.53, MMD = 1.54, and JSD = 3.59, and generalizes strongly to the Fusion360 dataset (ACCT = 78.35) despite training only on DeepCAD (li2025cadllamaleveraginglarge pages 13-15).

**CADLLM** (Liao et al., 2025) combined with TCADGen dramatically reduces geometric error: TCADGen+CADLLM achieves CD = 3.12, MMD = 2.78, JSD = 8.38, down from DeepCAD's standalone CD of 169.93. Per-command prediction reaches ACC = 0.966, F1 = 0.947, and AUC = 0.962 (liao2025automatedcadmodeling pages 7-8).

### 4.4 Multimodal and Diffusion-Based Approaches

**CAD-MLLM** (Xu et al., 2024) is the first system capable of generating parametric CAD models conditioned on text, images, point clouds, or combinations thereof. It introduces topology-quality metrics (Segment Error, Dangling Edge Length, Self-Intersection Ratio) alongside standard geometric measures. On point-cloud reconstruction, it achieves Chamfer = 1.85 (×100), F-score = 90.88, and substantially better topological quality than baselines like Point2CAD and NVDNet—e.g., SIR = 1.31% versus NVDNet's much higher topological errors (SegE = 37.22, DangEL = 47.97). For image-conditioned generation, it reports Chamfer = 3.77 versus InstantMesh's 5.38 (xu2024cadmllmunifyingmultimodalityconditioned pages 9-10). The associated Omni-CAD dataset contains approximately 450K instances with multimodal annotations.

**Draw Step by Step** (Ma et al., 2024, CVPR) uses multimodal diffusion to reconstruct CAD construction sequences from point clouds, generating sequences with improved accuracy and generation diversity (xu2024cadmllmunifyingmultimodalityconditioned pages 9-10).

### 4.5 Agentic CAD Pipelines

**CAD-Assistant** (Mallis et al., 2025) proposes a general-purpose, training-free CAD agent using a tool-augmented VLLM as planner with CAD-specific tools (sketch parameterizer, rendering modules, cross-section generator) executing via the FreeCAD Python API. It iteratively assesses the impact of generated CAD commands on geometry and adapts subsequent actions. On the CQA benchmark, it achieves accuracy of 0.791 with GPT-4o (mallis2025cadassistanttoolaugmentedvllms pages 5-6). Schema-embedded JSON with point-based geometry parametrization significantly outperforms other serialization approaches (mallis2025cadassistanttoolaugmentedvllms pages 5-6, mallis2025cadassistanttoolaugmentedvllms pages 1-2).

**CAD-HLLM** (Zuo et al., 2025) introduces a hierarchical two-stage text-to-CAD system where a Plan Generator produces symbolic plans and a Parameter Completer expands each into parameterized CAD sequences. Candidate selection uses a composite log-likelihood score with validity filtering through OpenCascade (zuo2025cadhllmgeneratingexecutable pages 7-9).

**CADDesigner** (Fan et al., 2025) uses a general-purpose LLM/VLM agent with expressive high-level CAD operations (revolve, arrays, patterns) and achieves the best overall performance and prompt-result alignment in evaluations against Text2CAD and other baselines (fan2025caddesignerconceptualdesign pages 8-9).

## 5. Cross-Cutting Constraints and Limitations

### 5.1 Manufacturability

Topology-optimization outputs frequently produce organic/asymmetric shapes that are difficult to manufacture conventionally and require post-processing (smoothing, reconstruction) that may compromise embedded manufacturing constraints (belluomo2025generativedesignperspectives pages 30-34). AI-generated CAD models face similar issues: unsupported overhanging features, thin walls below minimum thickness, intersecting geometries, and stress hotspots must be detected explicitly—defect detectors achieve approximately 89% precision but do not replace full FEA validation (bhosale2025aienhancedcomputeraideddesign pages 6-9). Additive-manufacturing-specific constraints (layer-by-layer feasibility, thermal distortion, support minimization, material anisotropy) must be modeled early to ensure outputs are manufacturable (bhosale2025aienhancedcomputeraideddesign pages 6-9). Approximately 10% of AI-generated CAD designs may be infeasible boundary representations.

### 5.2 Editability

Mesh-based outputs from text-to-3D tools are "not easily editable" and "often lack the precision and structural coherence for functional use in CAD environments" (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 40-44). In contrast, parametric sequence approaches (DeepCAD, CAD-Llama, Text-to-CadQuery) produce operation sequences or executable code that preserves editability and can be opened and modified in CAD software (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 21-25). However, generative design outputs often lack the parametric feature-history and traceability that parametric CAD provides (islam2025integratinggenerativedesign pages 3-5, buga2025artificialintelligencein pages 5-6).

### 5.3 Parametric Controllability

Current LLMs lack explicit geometric reasoning and spatial understanding, "merely predict statistically likely token sequences," leading to performance degradation as target complexity increases (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 25-29). Complex assemblies must still be designed part by part, and LLMs "struggle with the spatial relationships between different parts" (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 61-64). Agentic feedback pipelines (VLM + verifier loops) improve fidelity but remain constrained by iteration limits, language variability, and prompt sensitivity (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 25-29). Tasks requiring complex mathematical geometry (e.g., involute gears) remain particularly challenging (fan2025caddesignerconceptualdesign pages 8-9).

### 5.4 Scriptability for CI/CD

CadQuery offers "Excellent (Jupyter, scripting)" integration and is well-suited for automated pipelines, while OpenSCAD provides deterministic scripting but weaker ecosystem integration (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). A critical challenge for LLM-driven CI/CD is output variability: "the code that ChatGPT generates is not identical" across runs, reducing the reliability required for automated pipelines (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 40-44). Insufficient integration between MBSE tools and domain-specific toolchains (CAD/CAE, requirements management, simulation) further constrains end-to-end automation, and "human experts remain central in validation and decision-making" (molitorUnknownyearaiinnew pages 45-48).

## 6. Summary and Outlook

The generative CAD landscape as of 2025 is characterized by three parallel tracks converging toward integrated, AI-augmented design: mature commercial TO/GD tools offering simulation-validated, multi-variant design exploration; code-first parametric frameworks enabling reproducible, scriptable CAD pipelines; and a rapidly advancing research front in AI/LLM-driven generation that is improving geometric fidelity (Text-to-CadQuery: 48.6% CD reduction over Text2CAD; TCADGen+CADLLM: CD = 3.12 versus DeepCAD's 169.93), multimodal conditioning (CAD-MLLM: F-score 90.88 from point clouds), and agentic autonomy (CAD-Assistant, CADDesigner) (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 1-3, liao2025automatedcadmodeling pages 7-8, xu2024cadmllmunifyingmultimodalityconditioned pages 9-10, fan2025caddesignerconceptualdesign pages 8-9). Key open challenges include robust manufacturability enforcement, consistent parametric editability, spatial reasoning in LLMs, topological validity of generated models, and reliable integration into CI/CD engineering workflows. The field is moving toward systems that combine LLM reasoning, visual feedback, tool-augmented agents, and executable code-based CAD representations to bridge the gap between generative exploration and engineering-grade parametric design.
