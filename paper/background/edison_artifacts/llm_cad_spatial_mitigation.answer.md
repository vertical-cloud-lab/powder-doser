## 2. Documented Failure Modes

### 2.1 Parts Modelled in Isolation / Missing Boolean Unions

LLMs commonly generate individual geometric features—bosses, brackets, mounting tabs—as standalone solids that are never Boolean-unioned with the host body. This produces geometrically valid but functionally meaningless assemblies where features "float" in space. Kilpeläinen (2025) documents that LLMs frequently omit key geometric features such as boreholes from flanges, and produce simple-but-wrong simplifications (e.g., generating a circular profile instead of a hexagon), indicating fundamental failures in compositional spatial reasoning (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeaia pages 25-29, kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeai pages 25-29). The CadQuery fluent-API design, with its implicit internal context passing between chained operations, exacerbates this problem because LLMs struggle to track hidden state across long method chains (fan2025caddesignerconceptualdesign pages 4-5). CADDesigner's Explicit Context Imperative Paradigm (ECIP) was specifically introduced to make dependencies visible and reduce this class of errors (fan2025caddesignerconceptualdesign pages 4-5).

### 2.2 Wrong-Direction Boolean Cuts, Extrudes, and Workplane Errors

Orientation and direction errors are among the most persistent failure modes. Ocker et al. (2025) explicitly note that "VLMs still seem to struggle especially with the orientation of surfaces, e.g., when selecting suitable workplanes for sketches" (ocker2503fromideato pages 5-8). In their multi-agent experiments, iterative user feedback was required to correct errors such as wheels extruded in the wrong plane and asymmetric extrusions that needed `both=True` directionality corrections (ocker2503fromideato pages 5-8). CADSmith identifies bounding-box errors by millimeters, missing faces, and incorrect face/edge/vertex counts as common topology faults, many of which stem from wrong-direction operations (barkley2603cadsmithmultiagentcad pages 1-2). Xie and Ju (2025) document that LLM-generated CadQuery code using `threePointArc()` with nearly-colinear control points triggers OpenCASCADE kernel runtime failures, illustrating how numerically degenerate configurations arise from the model's lack of geometric intuition (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 15-18).

### 2.3 Arithmetically Inconsistent Clearance/Fit/Tolerance Values

The Zhang et al. (2026) survey in ACM Computing Surveys identifies that "LLMs still face difficulties in producing perfectly accurate parametric sequences," with models struggling to generalize to new shapes and primitives (zhang2026largelanguagemodels pages 16-18). Känsälä (2026) documents that LLMs misinterpret dimensions, inconsistently fill missing parameter details, and show sensitivity to prompt phrasing—all of which can produce clearance and tolerance values that are internally contradictory (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62). CADSmith reports "false convergence" scenarios where kernel metrics appear acceptable while the shape is fundamentally wrong, including cases with "subtle gaps between the arms and the central hub" that evade both programmatic checks and visual inspection (barkley2603cadsmithmultiagentcad pages 6-7).

### 2.4 Missing Functional Paths and Physical Interference

The broader spatial reasoning literature confirms that current LLMs have limited 3D awareness. Mouselinos et al. (2024) demonstrate a notable geometric reasoning gap: LLMs exhibit biases in target variable selection and struggle with 2D spatial relationships, often misrepresenting and hallucinating objects and their placements (zhang2026largelanguagemodels pages 14-16). ArtiCAD (Shui et al., 2026) explicitly argues that deferring assembly-relationship decisions to a late stage "forces a second planning pass that must parse all generated code, infer coordinate systems, and resolve cross-part dimensions—a task with long context and high failure rate" (shui2026articadarticulatedcad pages 3-5). This directly causes missing functional paths such as motor clearance violations, blocked inlets, and inaccessible fastener locations.

### 2.5 Vendor-Part Envelopes Ignored or Misremembered

Känsälä (2026) finds that retrieval-augmented generation was limited because the vector database "had no documents and only a gear-specific library consistently helped," meaning that vendor-component specifications were effectively unavailable to the model (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62). The recommended mitigation is to populate the RAG subsystem with CAD documentation, parametric examples, design rules, and robust component libraries (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62, kansala2026generativeaidrivensystemsa pages 59-62). The absence of verified mechanical-component libraries analogous to KiCad footprint databases remains a significant gap identified across multiple sources (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 30-32, wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 6-7).

### 2.6 Underspecified Fastener Strategy

While no single paper focuses exclusively on fastener-strategy failures, multiple sources document the broader pattern: LLMs generate geometry without encoding manufacturing constraints. The Wu et al. (2026) survey notes that generated models often "lack guaranteed validity, precision, manufacturability, and physical correctness required by industry" (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 1-3). The Känsälä thesis recommends incorporating manufacturing engineer agents with RAG-grounded access to design-for-additive-manufacturing (DfAM) rules to address this class of error (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62).

## 3. Quantitative Benchmarks

Several key quantitative results anchor the field:

- **CADSmith** achieves 100% execution success, median IoU of 0.9629, and reduces mean Chamfer Distance from 28.37 to 0.74 (~38× improvement) through its multi-agent pipeline with programmatic geometric validation (barkley2603cadsmithmultiagentcad pages 1-2).
- **CADCodeVerify** (Alrashedy et al., 2025) reports GPT-4 few-shot success rates of 96.5% on CadQuery code compilation, with IoGT reaching 0.944 (alrashedy2024generatingcadcode pages 7-9).
- **CAD-Coder** (Doris et al., 2026) achieves a 100% valid syntax rate when fine-tuned on 163K+ CadQuery-image pairs (doris2026cadcoderanopensource pages 7-8).
- **Text-to-CadQuery** (Xie & Ju, 2025) reaches 69.3% top-1 exact match with F1 median of 0.9836 and volumetric IoU median of 0.9868; a fine-tuned GPT-4.1 mini achieves a 0% invalid rate (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 8-10).

- **CADReview/ReCAD** (Chen et al., 2025) finds that GPT-4o achieves only 41.5% accuracy on human-made program error detection, and closed-source MLLMs produce invalid OpenSCAD code at rates exceeding 25% (chen2025cadreviewautomaticallyreviewing pages 8-9).

### 4.A Tool-Side Guardrails

The most effective tool-side guardrails combine deterministic geometry-kernel verification with learning-based repair. CADSmith's use of OpenCASCADE (OCCT) kernel-level checks—bounding-box dimensions, volume, face/edge/vertex counts, and solid validity—provides millimeter-precision feedback that image-only judging cannot achieve (barkley2603cadsmithmultiagentcad pages 1-2). CADCrafter integrates non-differentiable code-checker feedback into training via Direct Preference Optimization, penalizing sequences that fail to compile or produce invalid B-reps and achieving an Invalid Rate of only 3.6% (chen2025cadcraftergeneratingcomputeraided pages 6-7). The Wu et al. (2026) survey emphasizes that many AI-generated models contain geometric/topological flaws (self-intersections, gaps, non-manifold edges) that make them non-watertight and unusable for downstream manufacturing or FEA, underscoring the need for automated watertightness and manifold checks as basic admissibility filters (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 30-32). GenCAD-Self-Repairing demonstrates that latent-space diffusion guidance can repair approximately two-thirds of infeasible outputs without significantly altering the generative distribution (tsuji2025gencadselfrepairingfeasibilityenhancement pages 5-7).

### 4.B Prompting and Agent Strategies

Multi-agent architectures with independent visual critics represent the current state-of-the-art mitigation. CADSmith's five-agent pipeline (Planner, Coder, Executor, Validator, Refiner) with an independent VLM Judge achieves the largest reported improvements; critically, ablations show that removing the three-view rendered image increases Tier-3 mean Chamfer Distance from 1.42 to 49.68, demonstrating that visual feedback is essential for complex parts (barkley2603cadsmithmultiagentcad pages 6-7). CADCodeVerify implements a VQA-style visual chain-of-thought where the VLM generates and answers validation questions about rendered geometry, achieving a 5% absolute improvement in compilation success rate for GPT-4 (alrashedy2024generatingcadcode pages 7-9). ArtiCAD introduces a principled decomposition for articulated assemblies: predicting connectors (attachment points with local frames, semantic labels, and joint types) at design time rather than assembly time, thereby bypassing the spatial-reasoning limitations of current LLMs during the most error-prone phase (shui2026articadarticulatedcad pages 3-5). The Känsälä thesis warns, however, that multi-agent refinement loops can converge prematurely, with "most runs terminating after a single iteration or stopping at the iteration limit without resolving the identified issue" (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62).

### 4.C Representation Choices

The field is converging on code-based (CadQuery/Python) representations over task-specific command-sequence tokens. Text-to-CadQuery demonstrates that generating executable Python code leverages pretrained code-generation abilities and naturally benefits from future advances in foundation models without modifying the output design (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 8-10). The Wu et al. survey catalogs the representation landscape: Constructive Solid Geometry (CSG) captures construction logic via Boolean trees but requires kernel evaluation; Boundary Representation (B-rep) encodes topology with full precision but is challenging for neural networks; sketch-and-extrude sequences are structured and widely used but limited to a small operation vocabulary that excludes lofts, sweeps, and shells (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 6-7, wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 3-4). Key benchmark datasets include DeepCAD (178,238 command sequences), ABC (1M+ B-rep models), Fusion 360 Gallery (8,625 human-authored build histories), and SketchGraphs (15M constrained 2D sketches) (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 6-7, lin2025asurveyon pages 11-13). A critical gap is the lack of paired sketch↔B-rep datasets, which limits training on end-to-end parametric-to-solid workflows (heidari2025geometricdeeplearning pages 22-24, heidari2025geometricdeeplearning pages 24-24).

### 4.D Human-in-the-Loop Patterns

Ocker et al. (2025) implement a two-tier feedback architecture: an inner automated verification loop where a QA agent compares seven rendered views against the specification, and an outer human validation loop where the user provides corrective natural-language feedback (ocker2503fromideato pages 5-8). Their experiments show that the system often cannot converge autonomously on complex parts, making human review a practical necessity for engineering-grade outputs (ocker2503fromideato pages 5-8). Sadik and Bujny (2025) formalize the human-in-the-loop evaluation with a suite of quantitative metrics (volumetric similarity, surface similarity, Hausdorff distance, PCA alignment, ICP alignment) and show that code-level correction prompts—where the human edits the parametric source directly—achieve near-perfect reconstruction including zero Hausdorff distance, dramatically outperforming image-only prompt modalities (sadik2509humanintheloopquantitativeevaluation pages 4-5, sadik2509humanintheloopquantitativeevaluation pages 1-2). This finding has direct implications for the cost/benefit of fully autonomous vs. supervised generation: for hardware destined for 3D printing and assembly, the evidence strongly suggests that human review of multi-view renders and dimensioned parametric code, followed by edits fed back into the parametric source, remains necessary for functional correctness. ArtiCAD's Review Agent, which inspects multi-view renders of individual parts and keyframe sequences of joint motions, represents an intermediate automation level that reduces but does not eliminate human oversight (shui2026articadarticulatedcad pages 3-5).

Beyond the primary 16 sources, the following landmark works provide foundational context. **DeepCAD** (Wu et al., 2021) introduced the autoregressive sketch-and-extrude generation paradigm and the widely-used 178K-model dataset, but cannot guarantee that generated models are valid (kilpelainen2025utilizinggenerativeaia pages 25-29). **SkexGen** uses disentangled codebooks for CAD construction sequence generation and is a benchmark baseline across multiple newer papers (heidari2025geometricdeeplearning pages 22-24). **CADCrafter** (Chen et al., 2025, CVPR) introduced DPO-based training for CAD generation using latent diffusion and is among the first to bridge synthetic-to-real generalization (chen2025cadcraftergeneratingcomputeraided pages 6-7). **SceneCraft** (Hu et al., 2024) demonstrates LLM-based 3D scene synthesis as Blender code and is cited alongside **BlenderBench** as benchmarks for code-based 3D generation evaluation. **3DSRBench** (Ma et al., 2025) provides 2,772 manually annotated spatial reasoning QA pairs across 12 question types, revealing that current LMMs degrade on uncommon camera viewpoints and multi-object reasoning. ## 6. Open Problems and Future Directions

Several critical gaps remain unaddressed in the literature:

1. **Assembly-level verification**: Most current work focuses on single-part geometric fidelity. Interference/clash detection across multi-part assemblies, clearance verification, and functional-path checking (e.g., confirming that an inlet is unobstructed) lack standardized benchmarks or automated tools integrated with LLM pipelines.

2. **Manufacturing-aware validation**: DRC-style design rule checks for additive manufacturing (wall thickness, overhang angle, bridging distance, support requirements), slicer-side printability analysis, and FEA-in-the-loop validation are mentioned as future directions in surveys (wu2026aidrivengenerationof pages 30-32) but are not yet integrated into any published LLM-CAD pipeline.

3. **Fastener and joining strategy**: No published system reasons about self-tapping screws vs. heat-set inserts vs. through-bolts as a function of material, print orientation, and loading. This remains a gap requiring either domain-specific knowledge graphs or specialized engineering agents.

4. **Verified component libraries**: The mechanical-CAD community lacks the equivalent of KiCad's footprint libraries—verified vendor-component envelopes (motor datasheets, bearing bores, connector cutouts) that an LLM agent could query. RAG over unstructured datasheets is the current workaround but is unreliable (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62).

5. **Scaling to complex assemblies**: Nearly all benchmarks evaluate single parts with fewer than ~20 construction operations. Real mechanical assemblies with dozens of parts, kinematic chains, and cross-part dimensional dependencies remain largely untested. ArtiCAD's 120-task articulated benchmark is a first step but remains limited in scope (shui2026articadarticulatedcad pages 3-5).

6. **Robust evaluation metrics**: Current metrics (Chamfer Distance, IoU, F1) operate on point clouds or voxels and do not capture functional correctness (e.g., "does the shaft fit through the bearing bore?"). Test-driven CAD—writing assertions on volume, mass, bounding box, hole counts, and interference-free assemblies—is discussed informally but lacks a standardized framework (kansala2026generativeaidrivensystems pages 59-62).

## 7. Conclusion

The 2023–2026 literature establishes that LLMs and VLMs can generate syntactically valid CadQuery/OpenSCAD code at high rates (up to 100% valid syntax with fine-tuning) and achieve reasonable geometric fidelity on simple parts (median IoU > 0.96 in best systems). However, systematic failures persist in spatial reasoning, orientation/direction correctness, parametric consistency, and functional completeness—precisely the failure modes observed empirically in Claude/GPT-class models generating CadQuery code. The most effective mitigation strategies combine deterministic geometry-kernel verification (OCCT topology checks, watertightness filters) with multi-agent architectures employing independent visual critics and iterative self-repair loops. For hardware destined for 3D printing and physical assembly, the evidence consistently indicates that human review remains essential, particularly at the parametric-source level where code-level corrections achieve near-perfect fidelity. The field's next frontier lies in scaling these approaches to multi-part assemblies, integrating manufacturing-aware validation, and establishing verified mechanical-component libraries that ground LLM reasoning in physical reality.
