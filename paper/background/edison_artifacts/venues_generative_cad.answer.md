# Venue Analysis for a Generative-CAD / Open-Hardware Self-Driving Laboratory Contribution

## 1. The Publication Landscape for Generative-CAD Work

Generative-CAD research—including parametric sequence generation, B-rep synthesis, and LLM-driven text-to-CAD—is published across a broad spectrum of venues. Evidence from recent literature shows that the highest-profile generative-CAD *methods* papers appear in top ML and computer vision venues: CVPR hosted CAD-Llama (Li et al., 2025) for LLM-based parametric CAD generation (li2025cadllamaleveraginglarge pages 8-9), NeurIPS published Text2CAD (Khan et al., 2024) (dong2025histcadgeometricallyconstrained pages 12-12, le2025cadknittercompositionalcad pages 16-17), ICML published SkexGen (Xu et al., 2022) (le2025cadknittercompositionalcad pages 16-17, xu2024brepgenabrep pages 11-12), ICLR has OBJ2CAD-type work under review (houUnknownyearobj2cadobjectorientedtexttocad pages 1-2), and ACM Transactions on Graphics (SIGGRAPH) published BrepGen (Xu et al., 2024) (xu2024brepgenabrep pages 11-12). Applied code-based generative-CAD work using frameworks like CadQuery and OpenSCAD has appeared in engineering journals: Daareyni et al. (2025) published an LLM-to-OpenSCAD pipeline in The International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (daareyni2025generativeaimeets pages 9-10, daareyni2025generativeaimeets pages 1-2), Schüpbach et al. (2025) published an agentic LLM-CAD framework in Proceedings of the Design Society (gomez2025designautomationtechniques pages 86-89), and the Text-to-CadQuery system (Xie & Ju, 2025) demonstrates CadQuery-specific LLM fine-tuning (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15, xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 10-12).

Separately, self-driving laboratory (SDL) hardware contributions—particularly open-source, 3D-printed, and modular instruments—are concentrated in Digital Discovery (RSC), which has published open-hardware SDL platforms including the digital pipette (Yoshikawa et al., 2023) and democratized 3D-printed lab automation (Doloi et al., 2025) (doloi2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 29-30, doloi2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 1-2), as well as in HardwareX, which accepts detailed open-source hardware descriptions (lavayssiere2022laboremboxa pages 1-2). Other outlets for SDL hardware include Nature Communications, Chemical Reviews, and domain-specific chemistry/materials journals (pelkie2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 16-17, silva2024shapingthefuture pages 12-14, wenzel2023openhardwarefrom pages 25-28).

## 2. The Core Trade-Off: Applied Hardware/SDL vs. Pure Methods

The critical trade-off for this project is that the contribution is primarily an *applied* hardware/SDL system paper—a multi-powder doser for autonomous AM alloy discovery—that *uses* generative-CAD (LLM/AI-driven code-based CAD via CadQuery/build123d/OpenSCAD, with agentic design loops and geometry-kernel verifiers) as a design methodology, rather than advancing generative-CAD methods per se. This distinction fundamentally shapes venue selection:

**Venues that would NOT accept the paper** (high-impact but methods-focused): ACM TOG/SIGGRAPH, CVPR, ICCV/ECCV, NeurIPS, ICML, and ICLR all publish generative-CAD work, but they require the primary contribution to be a novel ML, vision, or graphics *method*. An applied paper that uses LLM-CAD to design hardware but does not advance the underlying model architecture, training procedure, or representation would almost certainly be desk-rejected at these venues (li2025cadllamaleveraginglarge pages 8-9, xu2024brepgenabrep pages 11-12).

**Venues that WOULD accept the paper** (applied-systems-friendly): Digital Discovery, IJAMT, ASME JMD/JCISE, Additive Manufacturing, Advanced Engineering Informatics, Computers in Industry, and Proceedings of the Design Society all have track records of publishing applied AI-for-design or hardware/SDL contributions, provided there is sufficient methodological or engineering novelty (doloi2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 1-2, daareyni2025generativeaimeets pages 9-10, gomez2025designautomationtechniques pages 86-89, man2025fromsketchto pages 84-84).

## 4. Top Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the recommended publication strategy depends on how the manuscript is framed:

### Primary Recommendation: Digital Discovery (RSC)
Digital Discovery is the single venue that sits at the intersection of SDL open hardware, AI-driven automation, and accessible 3D-printed instruments for materials discovery. It has published open-hardware SDL platforms (doloi2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 29-30, doloi2025democratizingselfdrivinglabs pages 1-2, maffettoneUnknownyearwhatismissing pages 1-3), has a growing IF (~6.2), and would welcome a paper that foregrounds the multi-powder doser as an AI-designed, open-source SDL module for autonomous AM alloy discovery. The generative-CAD pipeline would serve as the enabling methodology rather than needing to be a standalone ML contribution.

### High-Impact Alternative: The International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology (Springer)
IJAMT has direct precedent for LLM-driven code-based CAD work applied to manufacturing: Daareyni et al. (2025) published an LLM-to-OpenSCAD pipeline with manufacturing validation in this exact journal (daareyni2025generativeaimeets pages 9-10, daareyni2025generativeaimeets pages 1-2). A paper framing the doser as a case study in LLM-driven design-for-additive-manufacturing with fabrication and functional validation would be highly competitive here.

### If Emphasizing Design Methodology: ASME Journal of Mechanical Design or JCISE
If the manuscript can make a genuine methodological contribution regarding the agentic design loop, geometry-kernel verification, or DfAM-aware generative-CAD workflow, JMD or JCISE would provide higher prestige within the engineering design community. JMD has published the deep generative models review by Regenwetter et al. (2022) and GenCAD=3D (2025) (man2025fromsketchto pages 84-84), establishing precedent for AI-enabled design synthesis work.

### If Emphasizing Manufacturing/AM Outcomes: Additive Manufacturing (Elsevier)
With an IF of ~11, Additive Manufacturing is the highest-impact manufacturing-specific venue. However, the paper would need to center on AM-relevant contributions (novel powder dosing mechanisms, multi-material process capabilities, alloy discovery results) rather than the CAD pipeline itself.

### Complementary Hardware Paper: HardwareX
For maximum reproducibility impact, a companion HardwareX paper providing the full open-hardware documentation (CAD files, BOM, assembly instructions, validation data) would complement a more methodologically focused flagship paper in one of the venues above (lavayssiere2022laboremboxa pages 1-2).

### Conference Option: Proceedings of the Design Society (ICED/DESIGN Conference)
For rapid dissemination, the Design Society conferences (ICED, DESIGN) accept applied LLM-CAD pipeline papers with engineering case studies, as demonstrated by Schüpbach et al. (2025) (gomez2025designautomationtechniques pages 86-89). This is lower-impact but highly amenable to the described contribution.

## 5. Venues to Avoid for This Paper

ACM Transactions on Graphics, CVPR, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, and ICCV/ECCV should be avoided unless the manuscript can be reframed around a fundamentally new generative-CAD method (e.g., a novel agentic architecture with broadly applicable improvements over existing text-to-CAD systems, validated on multiple design domains beyond the doser). As currently described—an applied hardware system using generative-CAD as a tool—these venues would reject the paper on scope grounds (li2025cadllamaleveraginglarge pages 8-9, le2025cadknittercompositionalcad pages 16-17, xu2024brepgenabrep pages 11-12, houUnknownyearobj2cadobjectorientedtexttocad pages 1-2).
