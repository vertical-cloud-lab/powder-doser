# State-of-the-Art Landscape of Generative CAD and Generative Design Tools and Research (2025)

## 1. Introduction

The convergence of topology optimization (TO), generative design (GD), parametric code-based CAD, and AI/LLM-driven generation is reshaping how engineers and designers create 3D models. This landscape review covers four major pillars: (a) commercial TO/GD products, (b) parametric/code-based CAD frameworks, (c) AI/LLM-driven CAD generation research, and (d) cross-cutting constraints and limitations. A structured review of over 14,000 publications found that 72.7% of relevant AI-in-GD papers appeared after 2021, reflecting rapid growth driven by advances in generative AI and computational resources (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 26-28, peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 1-3).

## 2. Commercial Topology-Optimization and Generative-Design Products

Commercial GD and TO tools encode physical rules and optimization algorithms to generate many candidate forms from user-specified constraints, helping avoid designer fixation and producing multiple design embodiments rapidly (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4). **Autodesk Fusion Generative Design** generates 3D part forms from geometric and force parameters, exploring many alternatives that users can visualize, simulate, and filter. In case studies it achieved significant mass reductions (e.g., 705 g to 586 g on drone components). Users can apply manufacturing constraints to bias solutions toward AM or subtractive processes (souvanhnakhoomman2025acomprehensivereview pages 5-6, nemme2022integratinggenerativedesign pages 4-8).

**nTopology (nTop)** employs field-driven implicit modeling and FEA-informed lattice structures, particularly suited to powder-based AM workflows. It can generate and manipulate lattice structures driven by simulation data, providing strong DFAM (Design for Additive Manufacturing) support (nemme2022integratinggenerativedesign pages 8-11).

**Siemens NX + Simcenter HEEDS** automatically generates and compares hundreds to thousands of optimized options, integrating NX CAD geometry with Simcenter CAE for solutions targeted at additive manufacturing. The combined workflow enables large-scale design-space exploration but carries higher enterprise workflow complexity (souvanhnakhoomman2025acomprehensivereview pages 5-6).

**Altair Inspire (OptiStruct)** is among the earliest commercial TO platforms. It removes material from a 3D CAD model to meet goals such as maximum stiffness with minimum mass. Practical challenges include translating voxelized TO outputs into manufacturable parametric CAD (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4, nemme2022integratinggenerativedesign pages 4-8).

**PTC Creo Generative Design** and **ANSYS Discovery** offer integrated simulation-driven concept generation, with PTC targeting Creo-native parametric geometry and ANSYS providing real-time physics feedback. Both face the common limitation that optimized shapes often require significant remodeling for production readiness (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4).

**Rhino + Grasshopper** provides a flexible visual parametric/algorithmic modeling environment with plugins for structural analysis (Karamba3D, Kangaroo) and evolutionary optimization (Wallacei, Galapagos). While extremely versatile, it requires substantial user expertise and does not provide turnkey certified engineering optimization (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4).

Across all commercial TO/GD tools, common limitations include sensitivity to input definitions (loads/boundaries), risk of converging to local optima, high computational cost for high-resolution problems, and difficulty translating voxel-based density fields into manufacturable parametric CAD (B-splines, contours) (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4). Additive manufacturing remains best suited to small-batch or niche production, which limits practical manufacturing options for some GD/TO outputs (nemme2022integratinggenerativedesign pages 4-8). A structured review found that light-weighting studies using GD/TO report >40–60% mass savings versus expert designs (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 1-3).

## 3. Parametric / Code-Based CAD Frameworks

Code-based CAD frameworks enable programmatic, reproducible, and version-controllable 3D modeling, making them particularly valuable for CI/CD pipelines and automated design workflows. The following table compares the major frameworks:

| Framework | Language | License/Type | Kernel | GUI Required? | Python Ecosystem Integration | CI/CD Suitability | Key Strengths | Key Limitations |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CadQuery | Python | Open-source library | OpenCascade (via OCCT/OCP) | No | Excellent; strong Jupyter and scripting workflow, with no separate CAD software required (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15) | High | Parametric, programmable solid modeling; readable Python API; good for automation, testing, batch generation, and export pipelines; strong fit for reproducible engineering code (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15) | Less interactive than mainstream GUI CAD; advanced surfacing/assemblies can require more coding expertise; manufacturability rules are not automatic |

*Table: This table compares major parametric and code-driven CAD frameworks on language, kernel, scripting ergonomics, and CI/CD suitability. It is useful for distinguishing pure code-first CAD stacks from cloud-native feature authoring and browser-oriented geometry tools.*

**CadQuery** is a Python library for parametric 3D modeling built on the OpenCascade (OCCT) kernel. It requires no separate software, uses pure Python, has low modeling difficulty, and offers "excellent" integration with the Python ecosystem (Jupyter, scripting). Its headless operation makes it highly suited for CI/CD pipelines and automated batch geometry generation (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). Recent work on Text-to-CadQuery has demonstrated that CadQuery provides the best trade-off between usability and flexibility for programmatic shape synthesis, and is "well-suited for programmatic shape synthesis" (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15).

**OpenSCAD** uses its own domain-specific language (DSL) with a CGAL-based CSG pipeline. It requires separate software for execution, has medium modeling difficulty, and shows no direct Python ecosystem integration (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). While deterministic and reproducible, its limited interoperability constrains its use in modern Python-centric automation workflows.

**build123d** is a modern Python library also built on the OCCT kernel. It serves as a spiritual successor to CadQuery with explicit algebra/mode-building APIs and strong type hints, making it well-suited for testable, CI/CD-integrated CAD generation.

**Onshape FeatureScript** is a proprietary language embedded within the Onshape cloud CAD platform. It enables custom feature authoring within a production-grade parametric system with robust collaboration and versioning. However, it is cloud-only and vendor-tied, limiting offline/local CI/CD workflows.

**JSCAD** provides JavaScript-based parametric CSG modeling that runs in browsers and Node.js environments, making it accessible for web-based configurators and full-stack teams. It is less mature for heavy mechanical CAD than OCCT-based tools.

Key limitations across code-based frameworks include: (i) manufacturability rules are not automatically enforced—users must encode process constraints manually; (ii) interactive visual editing is weaker compared to GUI CAD systems; and (iii) complex freeform/NURBS surfacing can require substantial coding expertise.

### 4.1 Foundational Generative CAD Models

**DeepCAD** (ICCV 2021) pioneered autoregressive deep generative networks for sketch-and-extrude CAD program synthesis. On unconditional generation benchmarks, DeepCAD achieved Coverage (COV) = 65.46%, MMD = 1.29, JSD = 1.67, with 87.4% novel and 89.3% unique outputs, but only 46.1% of generated models were valid sealed B-rep solids. In autoencoder reconstruction, it reports command accuracy (ACCcmd) = 99.36 and parameter accuracy (ACCparam) = 97.47 with a median Chamfer Distance (CD) of 0.787 and an invalid ratio of 3.30% (lin2025asurveyon pages 16-18, lin2025asurveyon pages 14-16, lin2025asurveyon pages 18-19).

**SkexGen** (2022) introduced autoregressive generation of CAD construction sequences with a disentangled latent space separating sketch and extrusion properties. It substantially improved upon DeepCAD with COV = 84.74%, MMD = 1.02, JSD = 0.90, 99.1% novel and 99.8% unique outputs, and a human-perceived realism score of 46.9. For sketch generation, it achieved FID = 18.56 (xu2023hierarchicalneuralcoding pages 6-7, xu2023hierarchicalneuralcoding pages 13-20).

**HNC-CAD** (2023) extended controllable generation via hierarchical neural coding with VQ-VAE and cascaded autoregressive transformers, achieving CD = 1.09 (versus DeepCAD's 4.25) and an invalid ratio of only 0.32 in reconstruction benchmarks, enabling novel interaction capabilities for conditional generation and completion tasks (lin2025asurveyon pages 14-16).

**BrepGen** (CVPR 2024) addressed direct B-rep generation with COV = 71.26%, MMD = 1.04, JSD = 0.09, and 62.9% valid outputs—substantially improving validity over DeepCAD while maintaining 99.8% novelty and 99.7% uniqueness (lin2025asurveyon pages 16-18, lin2025asurveyon pages 18-19). More recent work by **BrepGPT** (2025) introduced a single-stage autoregressive framework using Voronoi Half-Patch representation for unified B-rep generation.

### 4.2 Text-to-CAD and LLM-Driven Generation

**Text-to-CadQuery** (2025) proposed generating executable CadQuery Python code directly from natural language, bypassing intermediate command sequences. The best fine-tuned model (Qwen2.5-3B) achieved 69.3% accuracy (up from 58.8% baseline), reduced Chamfer Distance by 48.6%, and attained a volumetric IoU median of 0.9868 and F1 median of 0.9836. The work demonstrated clear scaling benefits: even the smallest model (124M parameters) reached higher accuracy with only 58 minutes of training compared to 2 days for the Text2CAD baseline. A GPT-4.1 mini fine-tuned on 10k examples produced 0% invalid rate but did not exceed accuracy of the 3B model, highlighting that larger LLMs improve executability but require sufficient task-specific data to improve 3D generation accuracy (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 1-3, xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 8-10, xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 6-8).

**TCADGen + CADLLM** (2025) combined a Transformer-based CAD sequence generator with LLM-based refinement (Llama 3.2 3B), achieving a sequence-level accuracy of 0.864 and dramatically reducing Chamfer Distance from 120.99–169.93 (baseline transformers) down to approximately 3.12, representing orders-of-magnitude geometric improvement (liao2025automatedcadmodeling pages 7-8).

**CADCodeVerify** (2025) introduced iterative visual verification for LLM-generated CAD code. GPT-4 with few-shot prompting and CADCodeVerify achieved a median point-cloud distance of 0.127 and 96.5% success rate, outperforming Gemini 1.5 Pro (0.147, 85.0%) and CodeLlama (0.185, 73.5%) (alrashedy2024generatingcadcode pages 7-9).

**Seek-CAD** (2025) pioneered the use of locally deployed open-source inference LLMs (DeepSeek-R1) for training-free CAD parametric model generation with visual and chain-of-thought feedback in a self-refinement loop.

**CAD-Coder** (2025) fine-tuned DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B to generate CAD script codes with geometric annotations, achieving CD = 0.88 with comments versus 1.31 without, demonstrating the importance of annotation quality for LLM-based CAD generation (he2505cadcodertextguidedcadfiles pages 11-12).

### 4.3 Multimodal and Diffusion-Based Approaches

Diffusion models are increasingly applied to CAD generation. **RECAD** (AAAI 2025) employs dual diffusion networks generating raster sketches and extrusion boxes, offering advantages in continuous latent space interpolation and intuitive user control. **GenCAD-3D** (2025) uses multimodal latent space alignment with contrastive learning and latent diffusion models, augmented by synthetic dataset balancing (SynthBal), to improve reconstruction accuracy on high-complexity geometries (yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 1-2, yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 14-15).

A structured review identified GANs as the most common architecture for direct design generation (9/14 studies in a systematic sample), with conditional GANs incorporating physical parameters (stress fields, aerodynamic conditions) to produce more physically informed outputs. However, GAN outputs suffer from pixelation, aliasing, and fuzzy edges, and are generally used as starting points for downstream processing rather than final geometries (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 20-21, peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 23-24). Diffusion models are predicted to be adopted in engineering GD as they mature, though a representational mismatch persists between neural pixel/voxel formats and engineering-standard CAD formats (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 24-26).

### 4.4 Agentic CAD Pipelines

A framework for automating CAD generation using LLM agents with function calling and agent workflows evaluated five LLMs across four agent workflows. The agent workflow incorporating automated visual feedback outperformed others, especially with multimodal LLMs like ChatGPT-4o. A case study demonstrated its application to topology optimization and additive manufacturing with minimal human input (schupbach2025fromtextto pages 7-8, schupbach2025fromtextto pages 1-2).

## 5. Constraints and Limitations

### 5.1 Manufacturability

Across all approaches, manufacturability remains a critical under-addressed challenge. Commercial TO/GD tools can encode manufacturing constraints (e.g., AM build directions, minimum feature sizes), but outputs typically require extensive post-processing before they are production-ready (watson2023generativedesignof pages 2-4, nemme2022integratinggenerativedesign pages 4-8). AI-generated CAD models fundamentally "overlook key aspects such as manufacturability or the practical application of each geometry," with frameworks that focus on raw geometry creation lacking built-in DfM checks (schupbach2025fromtextto pages 7-8). Current AI approaches "reason about geometry only, without validation from a physical simulation," limiting confidence for functional and manufacturing requirements (liu2508breplerlanguageguidedediting pages 8-9). Research suggests integrating AI-based manufacturability checkers (e.g., surrogate models acting as MLP-based DfM filters) into late-stage optimization to improve producibility (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 24-26).

### 5.2 Editability and Parametric Controllability

A fundamental tension exists between AI-generated outputs and traditional parametric CAD workflows. Many generative models produce non-parametric representations (point clouds, meshes) that "require manual adjustments to meet exact design intent" and lack design history or CAD interoperability features essential for downstream modification (yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 1-2, schupbach2025fromtextto pages 1-2). Tokenization and quantization approaches used in sequence-based CAD generation produce coordinate precision loss and unstable predictions, causing misaligned or disconnected components (liu2508breplerlanguageguidedediting pages 8-9). Current methods do not explicitly capture high-level geometric constraints such as symmetry, orthogonality, or parallelism, so edits can break design intent (liu2508breplerlanguageguidedediting pages 8-9). The code-generation paradigm (e.g., Text-to-CadQuery) partially addresses editability by outputting executable parametric scripts, but this approach is currently limited to the sketch-and-extrude subset of CAD operations (yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 14-15).

### 5.3 Scriptability and CI/CD Integration

Code-based frameworks like CadQuery and build123d offer the strongest CI/CD integration due to their Python-native, headless operation and excellent scripting ecosystem support (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 12-15). OpenSCAD provides deterministic reproducibility but limited Python integration. Commercial TO/GD tools are largely GUI-bound with API access varying by vendor. The emerging Text-to-CadQuery paradigm bridges AI generation with CI/CD-friendly code output, leveraging the fact that LLMs already excel at Python generation (xie2505texttocadqueryanew pages 1-3).

### 5.4 Data and Benchmark Limitations

A critical limitation across AI-driven CAD is dataset imbalance: the standard DeepCAD dataset contains 178,238 models but only covers sketch-and-extrude programs, a small subset of real engineering geometries. High-complexity CAD programs are underrepresented, causing models to "excel on simple shapes but struggle with more intricate ones" (yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 1-2, yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 14-15). Standard evaluation metrics are "dominated by results on simple examples," potentially masking failures on complex industrial parts (yu2026gencadthreedimensionalcomputeraideddesign pages 1-2). Surrogate models face high data generation costs (reported in 56.8% of surveyed articles) and accuracy concerns (30% of articles), requiring trade-offs between training investment and performance (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 26-28, peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 21-23).

## 6. Summary and Outlook

The generative CAD landscape in 2025 spans a spectrum from mature commercial TO/GD platforms delivering proven mass-reduction results (>40–60% savings) through simulation-driven optimization, to rapidly advancing AI/LLM methods that are beginning to generate valid, editable parametric CAD from natural language. Code-based frameworks like CadQuery and build123d occupy a critical bridging role, enabling both traditional parametric modeling and emerging LLM-driven generation within CI/CD-compatible pipelines. Key remaining challenges include closing the gap between generated geometry and manufacturable, fully parametric CAD; expanding training data beyond simple sketch-and-extrude vocabularies; integrating physics-based validation into generative loops; and developing foundation surrogate models that generalize across engineering domains (peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 1-3, peckham2025artificialintelligencein pages 24-26). The convergence of diffusion models, agentic LLM pipelines, and structured CAD representations represents the most promising frontier for achieving production-grade AI-assisted design automation.
