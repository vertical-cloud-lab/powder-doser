# Automated and Generative Schematic/Circuit-Topology Synthesis and Netlist Generation for Electronic Design: A Literature Survey (2019–2025)

## Overview

This survey covers 15 key references spanning four methodological categories relevant to building a code-based generative pipeline for the control/driver electronics of an open-source modular multi-powder dosing device (microcontroller interfaces, stepper drivers, motor drivers, load-cell front-end amplifiers). The references are organized into: (a) machine-learning and generative models for analog/mixed-signal circuit topology generation, (b) LLM-based and agentic generation of schematics and netlists, (c) graph-neural-network (GNN) and reinforcement-learning (RL) approaches to circuit synthesis and device sizing, and (d) constraint-/specification-driven topology selection.

## Detailed Reference Summaries

### Category (a): ML and Generative Models for Analog Circuit Topology Generation

**1. Chang, C.-C., Shen, Y., Fan, S., Li, J., Zhang, S., Cao, N., Chen, Y., and Zhang, X. (2024). "LaMAGIC: Language-Model-based Topology Generation for Analog Integrated Circuits." arXiv:2407.18269.**

LaMAGIC pioneers the use of supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on a Flan-T5 (248M parameters) encoder-decoder model to generate analog circuit topologies from user-specified performance targets (e.g., voltage conversion ratio, efficiency) in a single forward pass (chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 1-2, chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 2-3). The system represents circuits as graphs encoded via adjacency matrices and develops multiple input/output formulations — including a float-input adjacency-matrix formulation (FM) — that align with the autoregressive nature of language models. Experimental results on power converter circuits demonstrate a success rate of up to 96% under a strict tolerance of 0.01 (chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 9-10, chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 1-2). The model is pretrained on 3–5 component circuits and transferred to 6-component designs using a dataset of 76K sampled circuits (chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 4-5). **Relevance to dosing device:** Demonstrates that specification-to-topology generation is feasible via LMs, a paradigm applicable to generating power converter sub-circuits (e.g., stepper driver power stages). **Limitations:** Restricted to small power converter topologies (3–6 passive/switching components); synthetic evaluation only; no direct applicability to mixed-signal blocks like load-cell amplifiers (chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 9-10, chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 4-5).

**2. Chang, C.-C., Lin, W.-H., Shen, Y., Chen, Y., and Zhang, X. (2025). "LaMAGIC2: Advanced Circuit Formulations for Language Model-Based Analog Topology Generation." arXiv:2506.10235.**

LaMAGIC2 introduces the Succinct Float-Input Canonical Formulation with Identifier (SFCI), which reduces token-length complexity from O(|V|²) to O(|V|) and adds component-type identifiers for better differentiation (chang2025lamagic2advancedcircuit pages 1-2). This yields 34% higher success rates under tight tolerance (0.01) and 10× lower MSE compared to the original LaMAGIC, with substantially shorter output sequences (~36–41 tokens vs. ~85–105) (chang2025lamagic2advancedcircuit pages 1-2, chang2025lamagic2advancedcircuit pages 3-5). **Relevance:** Improved scalability and precision make this formulation more practical for generating larger circuit blocks. **Limitations:** Still confined to power converter topologies; O(|V|) scaling, while improved, has not been demonstrated on complex mixed-signal circuits (chang2025lamagic2advancedcircuit pages 1-2, chang2025lamagic2advancedcircuit pages 3-5).

**3. Gao, J., Cao, W., Yang, J., and Zhang, X. (2025). "AnalogGenie: A Generative Engine for Automatic Discovery of Analog Circuit Topologies." arXiv:2503.00205.**

AnalogGenie is a GPT-based generative engine that represents circuits at the device-pin level using an Eulerian-circuit encoding of directed graphs, trained on a dataset of 3,350 distinct topologies across 11 circuit families (gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 5-7, gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 2-4). The pin-level representation captures low-level design details and scales to larger graphs better than adjacency-matrix methods. Data augmentation via multiple Eulerian serializations per topology yields ~70× more training data (gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 5-7). AnalogGenie demonstrates zero-shot generation of circuit types not present in training (e.g., transconductance amplifiers) and produces novel topologies validated in Cadence Virtuoso, including op-amps (GBW=12 MHz), DC converters (efficiency=0.95), and bandgap references (TC=3 ppm/°C) (gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 15-19). **Relevance:** The ability to discover novel topologies across diverse circuit families (including amplifiers and references) is directly applicable to generating load-cell front-end and voltage reference sub-circuits. **Limitations:** Requires a large curated dataset; specification conditioning is indirect (gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 2-4, gao2503analoggenieagenerative pages 1-2).

**4. Guo, T., Herber, D. R., and Allison, J. T. (2019). "Circuit Synthesis Using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)." AIAA Scitech 2019 Forum. DOI: 10.2514/6.2019-2350.**

This early work applies GAN variants (GAN, DCGAN, WGAN, improved WGAN) to generate circuit topologies encoded as adjacency matrices. Using datasets of 43K–123K enumerated topologies for frequency-response matching and low-pass filter realizability, the improved WGAN achieved the highest feasibility rate of up to 42.66% after 50,000 training iterations (guo2019circuitsynthesisusing pages 11-14, guo2019circuitsynthesisusing pages 14-17). **Relevance:** Establishes the foundational concept of using generative models for circuit topology search, relevant to exploring filter and signal-conditioning topologies. **Limitations:** Relatively low feasibility rates; graph isomorphism problems in fixed-size adjacency representations; no specification conditioning or manufacturability awareness (guo2019circuitsynthesisusing pages 2-4, guo2019circuitsynthesisusing pages 14-17).

**5. Poddar, S., Budak, A., Zhao, L., Hsu, C.-H., Maji, S., Zhu, K., Jia, Y., and Pan, D. Z. (2024). "A Data-Driven Analog Circuit Synthesizer with Automatic Topology Selection and Sizing." DATE 2024, pp. 1–6. DOI: 10.23919/DATE58400.2024.10546840.**

This work presents a modular, data-driven framework that combines a parameterized OPAMP topology library (~4,000 valid topologies, ~360 curated), VAE-based synthetic data augmentation, and DNN-Opt sizing to automatically select and size topologies from performance specifications (poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 1-2, poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 3-4). The system generates optimal-sized netlists in approximately 10 minutes using over 540K data points (poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 1-2, poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 5-6). **Relevance:** The modular library approach and specification-driven selection are directly applicable to choosing amplifier topologies for load-cell signal conditioning. **Limitations:** Requires a costly one-time data-collection effort; limited to OPAMP/OTA families (poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 1-2, poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 3-4).

### Category (b): LLM-Based and Agentic Generation of Schematics and Netlists

**6. Lai, Y., Lee, S., Chen, G., Poddar, S., Hu, M., Pan, D. Z., and Luo, P. (2025). "AnalogCoder: Analog Circuit Design via Training-Free Code Generation." arXiv:2405.14918.**

AnalogCoder is the first training-free LLM agent that reframes analog circuit design as Python (PySpice) code generation (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 1-2, lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 2-4). It uses domain-specific prompts, a four-stage feedback loop (requirement checks, simulation/operating-point checks, DC sweep checks, functional checks), and a circuit tool library that archives successful designs as reusable modular sub-circuits (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 26-28, lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 2-4). AnalogCoder solved 20 of 24 benchmark tasks, outperforming standard GPT-4o (15 solved) and Llama-3 (11 solved) (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 1-2, lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 2-4). **Relevance:** The PySpice code-generation paradigm and modular sub-circuit library are directly transferable to generating driver electronics: one could encode stepper-driver half-bridges, motor H-bridges, or instrumentation amplifier blocks as library components and compose them via LLM-generated code. **Limitations:** Struggles with highly complex circuits; emphasizes functional correctness over parameter optimization; requires external SPICE simulation for validation; a later evaluation (AnalogCoder-Pro) showed only 10% pass rate on harder specification-driven benchmarks (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 9-11, wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 6-7).

**7. Zhang, H., Sun, S., Lin, Y., Wang, R., and Bian, J. (2025). "AnalogXpert: Automating Analog Topology Synthesis by Incorporating Circuit Design Expertise into Large Language Models." ISEDA 2025, pp. 772–777. arXiv:2412.19824.**

AnalogXpert formulates topology synthesis as subcircuit-level SPICE code generation and incorporates circuit design expertise through a subcircuit library, chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting, in-context learning examples, and iterative human-experience-based proofreading (zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 2-4, zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 1-2). On a benchmark of 30 real and 2,000 synthetic tasks, AnalogXpert achieved 40% and 23% success rates respectively, markedly outperforming GPT-4o at 3%/3% (zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 2-4, zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 9-10). **Relevance:** The SPICE-level output and subcircuit decomposition align well with practical PCB-level design (e.g., decomposing a motor driver into gate driver, half-bridge, current sense subcircuits). **Limitations:** Connection errors and wrong block selections remain common failure modes; the real dataset is small (30 tasks) (zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 9-10).

**8. Vijayaraghavan, P., Shi, L., Degan, E., and Zhang, X. (2024). "CircuitSynth: Leveraging Large Language Models for Circuit Topology Synthesis." IEEE LLM Aided Design Workshop (LAD), pp. 1–6. DOI: 10.1109/LAD62341.2024.10691716.**

CircuitSynth introduces a two-phase pipeline: Phase 1 fine-tunes an LLM to generate netlist-encoded topologies autoregressively, and Phase 2 applies a RoBERTa-based validity classifier (F1 ≈ 92%) with Gumbel-softmax refinement to steer generation toward valid circuits (vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 1-2, vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 2-3). Trained on 862,606 power-converter examples, the best models achieved ~71–73% SPICE-validated topology validity (vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 3-4, vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 5-6). **Relevance:** The validity-classifier refinement concept could be adapted to filter LLM-generated netlists for driver electronics against electrical rule checks. **Limitations:** Restricted to power converters; no specification-conditioned generation (vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 1-2, vijayaraghavan2024circuitsynthleveraginglarge pages 5-6).

**9. Chen, P.-H., Lin, Y.-S., Lee, W., Leu, T.-Y., Hsu, P.-H., Dissanayake, A., Oh, S., and Chiu, C.-S. (2025). "MenTeR: A Fully-Automated Multi-Agent Workflow for End-to-End RF/Analog Circuits Netlist Design." IEEE ICLAD 2025, pp. 124–132. arXiv:2505.22990.**

MenTeR deploys a multi-agent architecture comprising a Primary Investigator (PI) agent for specification understanding, a Circuit Agent for netlist synthesis via Chain-of-Stage (CoS) reasoning, and a Testbench Agent for simulation validation using Diagram-Aware RAG (DA-RAG) (chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 2-4, chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 4-5). With GPT-4o as backbone, MenTeR achieved 84.2% Pass@1 and 89.2% Pass@5, supporting end-to-end design from specification to validated PySpice netlist and testbench (chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 7-8). **Relevance:** The multi-agent spec-to-netlist-to-testbench pipeline is the closest paradigm to what would be needed for an automated dosing-device electronics generator — one agent interprets requirements (e.g., "4 stepper channels, 24V, load-cell excitation"), another generates netlists, and a third validates via simulation. **Limitations:** AI-generated nets can contain subcircuit definition errors and missing wires at system level, necessitating human-in-the-loop review for complex designs (chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 7-8).

**10. Wang, Z., Gao, J., Fu, W., Guo, X., and Zhang, X. (2025). "AnalogSAGE: Self-Evolving Analog Design Multi-Agents with Stratified Memory and Grounded Experience." arXiv:2512.22435.**

AnalogSAGE coordinates three sequential agent stages — topology selection, topology refinement, and parameter optimization — within a closed-loop, simulation-grounded workflow augmented by four-layer stratified memory for long-horizon reasoning and cross-task knowledge transfer (wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 2-3, wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 1-2). Using SKY130 PDK and ngspice, AnalogSAGE reports a 10× overall pass-rate improvement and 48× Pass@1 improvement over baselines, with Bayesian optimization for parameter tuning (wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 1-2, wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 4-5). **Relevance:** The self-evolving, simulation-grounded approach with memory-based learning could enable iterative improvement of driver circuit designs across multiple dosing channels. **Limitations:** Currently demonstrated only on op-amp designs in SKY130; recent preprint with limited independent validation (wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 1-2).

**11. Bhandari, J., Bhat, V., He, Y., Garg, S., Rahmani, H., and Karri, R. (2024). "Masala-CHAI: A Large-Scale SPICE Netlist Dataset for Analog Circuits by Harnessing AI." arXiv:2411.14299.**

Masala-CHAI provides an automated pipeline for extracting SPICE netlists from circuit schematic images in textbooks, using YOLOv8 for component detection and deep Hough-transform priors for net recognition, followed by LLM-based netlist generation and verification (bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 1-2, bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 3-4). The open-sourced dataset of ~7,500 netlists from 10 textbooks enables LLM fine-tuning; models fine-tuned on Masala-CHAI improved AnalogCoder's Pass@1 by 46% (bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 1-2, bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 2-3). Structural correctness is verified via Graph Edit Distance (GED), achieving 100% similarity with AMSNet reference netlists (bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 7-9). **Relevance:** The dataset and pipeline could be extended to extract and catalog common control electronics circuits (H-bridges, stepper drivers, instrumentation amplifiers) from open-source hardware documentation, creating training data for a dosing-device-specific generator. **Limitations:** Component confusion (NMOS vs. PMOS) and weak transistor-level net detection (~45% accuracy without CV support) remain significant challenges (bhandari2024masalachaialargescale pages 2-3, tao2024amsnetnetlistdataset pages 1-2).

### Category (c): GNN and RL Approaches to Circuit Synthesis and Device Sizing

**12. Dong, Z., Cao, W., Zhang, M., Tao, D., Chen, Y., and Zhang, X. (2023). "CktGNN: Circuit Graph Neural Network for Electronic Design Automation." arXiv:2308.16406.**

CktGNN uses a two-level (nested) GNN architecture where inner GNNs encode subgraph-basis components and an outer directed GNN performs message passing over the transformed circuit DAG (dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 1-2, dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 4-6). Integrated into a VAE framework, CktGNN jointly addresses topology generation and device sizing. The authors release the Open Circuit Benchmark (OCB) with 10K distinct operational amplifiers. In experiments, CktGNN achieves ~91.9% valid-circuit generation rate, ~96.3% novelty, FoM Pearson correlation r ≈ 0.806, and outperforms human expert designs in Bayesian-optimization-guided search (dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 18-20, dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 9-11). **Relevance:** The GNN+VAE approach to joint topology-and-sizing optimization is applicable to amplifier blocks in a dosing device (e.g., load-cell instrumentation amplifiers, analog front-ends). The OCB benchmark and open-source code support reproducible research. **Limitations:** Benchmark is op-amp-centric with idealized component models; not directly demonstrated on mixed-signal driver circuits (dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 6-8, dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 2-4).

**13. Settaluri, K., Haj-Ali, A., Huang, Q., Hakhamaneshi, K., and Nikolic, B. (2020). "AutoCkt: Deep Reinforcement Learning of Analog Circuit Designs." arXiv:2001.01808.**

AutoCkt formulates analog circuit sizing as a multi-objective RL problem using Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), where the agent observes simulated performance and target specifications and iteratively adjusts device parameters (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 1-2, settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 2-3). Trained on only 50 target specifications, AutoCkt generalizes to 96.3% of 1,000 unseen targets with an average of 27 simulations to converge — approximately 40× faster than genetic algorithms (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 3-5). Transfer learning from schematic to post-layout (PEX) environments produced 40 LVS-passed operational amplifier layouts in TSMC 16nm (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 6-6, settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 5-6). **Relevance:** Once a topology is selected for a dosing-device sub-circuit (e.g., a transimpedance amplifier for load-cell readout), AutoCkt-style RL sizing can rapidly optimize component values to meet gain, bandwidth, and noise specifications. **Limitations:** Fixed-topology sizing only; requires access to a SPICE simulator and environment engineering; not a topology generator (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 1-2).

**14. Zhao, Z. and Zhang, L. (2022). "Deep Reinforcement Learning for Analog Circuit Structure Synthesis." DATE 2022, pp. 1157–1160. DOI: 10.23919/DATE54114.2022.9774699.**

This work is among the first to apply deep RL directly to analog circuit topology synthesis (rather than just sizing). A policy-gradient neural network (PGNN) incrementally constructs topologies by selecting building blocks from a predefined library, with hash tables and GPDD symbolic analysis used to efficiently filter and evaluate candidates before SPICE-based NSGA-II verification (zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 1-1, zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 3-4). The method requires substantially less computation than brute-force enumeration approaches like MOJITO (zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 4-4). **Relevance:** The building-block-based topology construction paradigm could be adapted for driver electronics by defining blocks such as half-bridges, gate drivers, current mirrors, and feedback networks. **Limitations:** Constrained by the predefined building-block library; stochastic results requiring multiple runs (2–9 hours each); downstream sizing still needed (zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 3-4, zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 4-4, zhao2022deepreinforcementlearning pages 1-2).

**15. Choi, Y., Park, S., Choi, M., Lee, K., and Kang, S. (2024). "MA-Opt: Reinforcement Learning-Based Analog Circuit Optimization Using Multi-Actors." IEEE TCAS-I, 71(5):2045–2056. DOI: 10.1109/TCSI.2024.3356582.**

MA-Opt uses multiple parallel RL actors with a shared elite-solution memory and a cooperative near-sampling strategy to optimize analog circuit device parameters. A critic network serves as a surrogate simulator to reduce expensive SPICE calls (choi2024maoptreinforcementlearningbased pages 1-2). MA-Opt achieves up to 34% better Figure-of-Merit than DNN-Opt at the same simulation budget and consistently satisfies all given performance constraints (choi2024maoptreinforcementlearningbased pages 1-2). **Relevance:** Multi-actor RL sizing is applicable to optimizing component values in motor driver and signal-conditioning circuits where multiple performance targets (efficiency, thermal, bandwidth) must be simultaneously satisfied. **Limitations:** Sizing-only; requires a valid starting topology and simulation-based supervision (choi2024maoptreinforcementlearningbased pages 1-2).

## Cross-Cutting Analysis: Capabilities, Limitations, and Relevance to a Dosing-Device Electronics Pipeline

### Capabilities
The surveyed methods collectively demonstrate that: (1) LM-based approaches can achieve high success rates (up to 96%) for specification-conditioned topology generation of well-defined circuit families (chang2024lamagiclanguagemodelbasedtopology pages 1-2); (2) training-free LLM agents like AnalogCoder and MenTeR can generate functional PySpice/SPICE netlists with multi-stage feedback verification, solving 20+/24 benchmark tasks (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 1-2, chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 7-8); (3) GNN+VAE methods enable joint topology-sizing optimization with >91% validity rates (dong2023cktgnncircuitgraph pages 18-20); and (4) RL-based sizing converges to meet specifications on 96%+ of unseen targets with 40× speedup over genetic algorithms (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 3-5).

### Hallucination and Validity
A recurring challenge across LLM-based methods is the generation of structurally invalid or electrically incorrect circuits. AnalogCoder requires multi-stage simulation-based feedback to catch floating nodes and incorrect operating points (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 26-28, zadeh2025generativeaifor pages 12-13). MenTeR found that AI-generated netlists sometimes passed automated benchmarks yet contained errors upon schematic inspection, including incorrectly defined subcircuits and missing wire connections (chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 7-8). AnalogXpert reports persistent connection errors and wrong block selections even with iterative proofreading (zhang2025analogxpertautomatinganalog pages 9-10). These findings underscore the necessity of SPICE-level simulation verification in any generative pipeline.

### Manufacturability
Most methods operate at the schematic level and do not address layout parasitics, PCB-level constraints, or component availability (zadeh2025generativeaifor pages 11-12, zadeh2025generativeaifor pages 12-13). AutoCkt is a notable exception, demonstrating transfer to post-layout (PEX) environments with LVS-passed designs (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 5-6). For a dosing device using discrete, off-the-shelf components (DRV8825 stepper drivers, HX711 load-cell ADCs, etc.), the gap between schematic-level generation and a manufacturable BOM/PCB is substantial — generated topologies would need to be mapped to specific component footprints, validated against datasheets, and checked for thermal and EMC compliance.

### Relevance to Dosing-Device Control Electronics
The control electronics for a multi-powder dosing device (microcontroller, stepper drivers, motor drivers, load-cell front-end) represent a system-level design challenge that spans digital control logic and multiple analog/mixed-signal sub-circuits. The most directly applicable paradigms are: (1) **Multi-agent agentic workflows** (MenTeR, AnalogSAGE) that decompose a system specification into sub-blocks and generate, validate, and iterate on each block's netlist (chen2025menterafullyautomated pages 2-4, wang2512analogsageselfevolvinganalog pages 1-2); (2) **Code-generation agents** (AnalogCoder) that produce executable PySpice code from natural-language descriptions, enabling composition of modular sub-circuits such as H-bridges and instrumentation amplifiers (lai2025analogcoderanalogcircuit pages 1-2); (3) **Specification-driven topology selectors** (Poddar et al.) that rank candidate topologies from a parameterized library against target specifications and automatically size them (poddar2024adatadrivenanalog pages 1-2); and (4) **RL-based optimizers** (AutoCkt, MA-Opt) for downstream component-value tuning once topologies are selected (settaluri2020autocktdeepreinforcement pages 1-2, choi2024maoptreinforcementlearningbased pages 1-2). A practical pipeline would likely combine these approaches: an LLM agent interprets system requirements and selects/generates topology candidates, a GNN or RL module optimizes device parameters, and simulation-in-the-loop verification catches hallucinated or invalid designs before output.

### Key Gaps
No surveyed work has demonstrated end-to-end generation of complete, manufacturable PCB-level control electronics from specifications. Current methods are largely validated on IC-level analog circuits (op-amps, power converters, bandgap references) rather than board-level mixed-signal systems with discrete components. Bridging this gap — from transistor-level IC topology to board-level system design with real component libraries — remains an open challenge for the field (zadeh2025generativeaifor pages 11-12, fang2025asurveyof pages 48-50).
