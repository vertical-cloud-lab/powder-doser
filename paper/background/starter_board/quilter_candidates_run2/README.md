# Quilter.ai routed candidate boards — run 2, 2026-07-14 (provenance)

The `candidate_N/` folders are the **auto-placed-and-routed** boards returned
by [Quilter.ai](https://www.quilter.ai) when @lbwinters re-ran the
`test_module_unplaced` trio generated by
[`../build_starter_board.py`](../build_starter_board.py) — this time the
**post-audit** board (110 × 110 mm outline, vendor-exact footprints for every
carrier, PR #100 scale integration: 15 footprints / 172 pads / 22 nets) with
the reviewed Quilter settings applied (JLCPCB 4-layer *with power plane*
JLC04161H-7628 stackup, 10 mil / 6 mil rules, per-net power currents, bypass-cap
assignments). Source: PR
[#76](https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/powder-doser/pull/76), the
2026-07-14 comment with six `Quilter_7_14_PCB_run_Candidate_N.zip` attachments.

Each folder holds Quilter's `test_module_unplaced.kicad_pcb` (placed + routed,
4-layer `F.Cu / In1.Cu (GND plane) / In2.Cu / B.Cu`) and the companion
`test_module_unplaced.kicad_pro`, committed **verbatim** as Quilter emitted
them.

`candidates_run2_copper.png` is a labeled montage of the five retrievable
candidates (top copper + outline + silk), rendered with
`kicad-cli pcb export svg`.

[`analyze_quilter_candidates_run2.py`](analyze_quilter_candidates_run2.py) is
the read-only, re-runnable analysis (track length, vias, KiCad DRC bucketed by
type, connectivity, per-net trace widths, **which side each part was placed
on**, and the systemic `<no net>` zone on `In2.Cu`); `analysis_summary_run2.json`
is its captured output.

[`simulate_power_ngspice_run2.py`](simulate_power_ngspice_run2.py) is the
read-only, re-runnable **ngspice** power-integrity simulation (resistor-mesh
IR-drop `.op` solve of the routed +12 V / +5 V / +3V3 copper + a transient
decoupling `.tran` of the +12 V solenoid rail). It upgrades the run-1 mesh
builder with T-junction splitting and layer-aware node clustering, and
detects nets whose continuity relies on a module's internal copper
(`module_internal_bridges` — see candidate 5's +12 V through SR1).
`analysis_power_ngspice_run2.json` is its captured output.
