❯ Should I tie the scale ground into the ground of the Pico W?                  

● Yes — and this is a genuinely important question, because a missing signal 
  ground is a classic cause of a dead-silent RS-232 link, exactly the symptom
  this thread has been chasing.

  Here's the reasoning. RS-232 is single-ended: every signal voltage is measured
  relative to signal ground. The scale's TXD swings to roughly −5…−9 V and
  +5…+9 V with respect to its own SG pin. If the module doesn't share that same
  ground reference, those voltages are floating/undefined at the receiver — you
  get silence or garbage, no matter how perfect TX and RX are.

  The key point: normally you should not need a separate ground wire, because
  the ground is supposed to travel through the cable:

  Scale SG (DB9 pin 5) ──cable pin 5──► Module DB9 pin 5 ──► Module GND
  ──(stacked)──► Pico GND

  Stacking already ties the module's GND to the Pico's GND. So if the DB9 cable 
  carries pin 5, the scale's ground is already tied in and you're done.

  But — your cable is the hand-made one (AutoTrickler connector cut off,
  extension wires soldered on). The single most likely gap there is that the
  SG/pin-5 conductor is missing or not landed on pin 5. People often wire only
  TX and RX and forget ground. So:

  1. First, verify it with the multimeter (continuity mode): probe from the
  scale's DB9 pin 5 to the Pico's GND. If it beeps/reads ~0 Ω, ground is already
  carried through the cable — leave it alone.
  2. If there's no continuity, then yes — explicitly tie them. Run a wire from
  the scale's signal ground (DB9 pin 5, or the scale-side SG conductor) to any
  Pico GND pin. That's a required RS-232 connection, not optional.

  Two cautions:
  - Tie signal ground only (DB9 pin 5 / the scale's SG line) — not
  chassis/earth, not any other pin. Getting the wrong conductor here is how a
  "ground" wire becomes a short.
  - A small ground-potential offset between the mains-powered scale and the
  USB-powered Pico is normal and fine — RS-232 is designed to tolerate several
  volts of it. Sharing signal ground is the correct fix, not a risk.

  So: check pin-5 continuity to Pico GND first. If it's already continuous,
  connect the scale and tell me. If it's not, add the ground tie, then connect
  and tell me — and there's a real chance that ground was the whole problem.

  Scale TXD  ──►  Module RXD   (the scale talks, the module listens)
